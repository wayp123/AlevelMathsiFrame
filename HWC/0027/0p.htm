Driving in built-up areas(0001).

Residential streets, 152 (0002).

You should drive slowly, and carefully on streets, where there are likely to be pedestrians, cyclists, and parked cars(0003). In some areas, a 20 mph (32 km-h) maximum speed limit may be in force(0004). Look out for(0005).

·         vehicles emerging from junctions, or driveways(0006).

·         vehicles moving off(0007).

·         car doors opening(0008).

·         pedestrians(0009).

·         children running out, from between parked cars(0010).

·         cyclists, and motor-cyclists(0011).

 

b0002

Traffic-calming measures, 153(0012).

On some roads there are features such as, road humps, chicanes (see drawing) and narrowings, which are intended to slow you down(0013). When you approach these features, reduce your speed(0014). Allow cyclists, and motor-cyclists, room to pass through them(0015). Maintain a reduced speed, along the whole of the stretch of road, within the calming measures(0016). Give way to oncoming road users, if directed to do so by signs(0017). You should not overtake, other moving road users, while in these areas(0018).

 

Country roads(0019).

154, (0020).

Take extra care on country roads, and reduce your speed at approaches to bends, which can be sharper than they appear, and at junctions, and turnings, which may be partially hidden(0021). Be prepared for pedestrians, horse riders, cyclists, slow-moving farm vehicles, or mud on the road surface(0022). Make sure you can stop, within the distance, you can see to be clear(0023). You should also reduce your speed, where country roads, enter villages(0024).

 

b0003

Single-track roads, 155(0025).

These are only wide enough for one vehicle(0026). They may have special passing places(0027). If you see a vehicle coming towards you, or the driver behind wants to overtake, pull into a passing place on your left, or wait opposite a passing place on your right(0028). Give way to vehicles coming uphill whenever you can(0029). If necessary, reverse, until you reach a passing place, to let the other vehicle pass(0030). Slow down, when passing pedestrians, cyclists, and horse riders(0031).

156, (0032).

Do not park in passing places(0033).

 

b0004

Vehicles prohibited from using roads, and pavements(0034).

157, (0035).

Certain motorised vehicles, do not meet the construction, and technical requirements for road vehicles, and are generally not intended, not suitable, and not legal for road, pavement, footpath, cycle path, or bridleway use(0036). These include most types of miniature motorcycles, also called mini motos, and motorised scooters, also called go-peds, which are powered by electric, or internal combustion engines(0037). These types of vehicle MUST NOT be used on roads, pavements, footpaths, or bridleways(0038).

Law (0039).

 

b0005

158, (0040).

Certain models of motorcycles, motor tricycles, and quadricycles, also called quad bikes, are suitable only for off-road use, and do not meet legal standards for use on roads(0041). Vehicles that do not meet these standards, MUST NOT be used on roads(0042). They MUST NOT be used on pavements, footpaths, cycle paths, or bridleways either(0043). You MUST make sure that, any motorcycle, motor tricycle, quadricycle, or any other motor vehicle, meets legal standards, and is properly registered, taxed, and insured, before using it on the roads(0044). Even when registered, taxed, and insured for the road, vehicles MUST NOT be used on pavements(0045).

Law (0046).
