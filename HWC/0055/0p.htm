Vehicle maintenance, safety, and security(0001).

Vehicle maintenance(0002).

Take special care that lights, brakes, steering, exhaust system, seat belts, demisters, wipers, and washers are all working(0003).

Also(0004).

·         lights, indicators, reflectors, and number plates MUST be_ kept clean, and clear(0005).

·         windscreens, and windows MUST be kept clean, and free from obstructions to vision(0006).

·         lights MUST be properly adjusted, to prevent dazzling other road users(0007). Extra attention needs to be paid to this, if the vehicle is heavily loaded(0008).

·         exhaust emissions MUST NOT exceed prescribed levels(0009).

·         ensure your seat, seat belt, head restraint, and mirrors, are adjusted correctly before you drive(0010).

·         ensure that items of luggage are securely stowed(0011).

Law (0012).

 

b0002

Warning displays_(0013).

Make sure that you understand the meaning of all warning displays, on the vehicle instrument panel(0014). Do not ignore warning signs, they could indicate a dangerous fault developing(0015).

·         when you turn the ignition key, warning lights will be illuminated but will go out when the engine starts {except the handbrake warning light}(0016). If they do not, or if they come on while you are driving, stop, and investigate the problem, as you could have a serious fault(0017).

·         if the charge warning light comes on while you are driving, it may mean that the battery isnt charging(0018). This should be checked, as soon as possible, to avoid loss of power to lights, and other electrical systems(0019).

Window tints(0020).

You MUST NOT use a vehicle with excessively dark tinting applied to the windscreen, or to the glass in any front window, to either side of the driver(0021). Window tinting applied during manufacture, complies with the Visual Light Transmittance {VLT} standards(0022). There are no VLT limits for rear windscreens, or rear passenger windows(0023).

Law (0024).

 

b0003

Tyres_(0025).

Tyres MUST be correctly inflated, to the vehicle manufacturers specification, for the load being carried(0026). Always refer to the vehicles handbook, or data(0027). Tyres should also be free from certain cuts, and other defects(0028).

Cars, light vans, and light trailers MUST_ have a tread depth of at least 1.6 milimeter across the central three-quarters, of the breadth, of the tread, and around the entire circumference(0029).

Motorcycles, large vehicles, and passenger-carrying vehicles MUST have a tread depth of at least 1 milimeter, across three-quarters, of the breadth of the tread, and in a continuous band around the entire circumference(0030).

Mopeds should have visible tread(0031).

Be aware that some vehicle defects can attract penalty points(0032).

Law (0033).

If a tyre bursts while you are driving, try to keep control of your vehicle(0034). Grip the steering wheel firmly, and allow the vehicle to roll to a stop, at the side of the road(0035).

If you have a flat tyre, stop as soon as it is safe to do so(0036). Only change the tyre if you can do so without putting yourself, or others at risk - otherwise call a breakdown service(0037).

 

b0004

Tyre pressures(0038).

Check weekly(0039). Do this before your journey, when tyres are cold(0040). Warm, or hot tyres may give a misleading reading(0041).

Your brakes, and steering will be adversely affected by under-inflated, or over-inflated tyres(0042). Excessive, or uneven tyre wear may be caused, by faults in the braking, or suspension systems, or wheels which are out of alignment(0043). Have these faults corrected as soon as possible(0044).

Fluid levels_(0045).

Check the fluid levels in your vehicle at least weekly(0046). Low brake fluid may result in brake failure, and a crash(0047). Make sure you recognise the low fluid warning lights if your vehicle has them fitted(0048).

Before winter(0049).

Ensure that the battery is well maintained, and that there are appropriate anti-freeze agents in your radiator, and windscreen bottle(0050).

 

b0005

Other problems(0051).

If your vehicle_(0052).

·         pulls to one side when braking, it is most likely to be a brake fault, or incorrectly inflated tyres(0053). Consult a garage, or mechanic immediately(0054).

·         continues to bounce after pushing down on the front, or rear, its shock absorbers are worn(0055). Worn shock absorbers can seriously affect the operation of a vehicle, and should be replaced(0056).

·         smells of anything unusual such as burning rubber, petrol, or an electrical fault; investigate immediately(0057). Do not risk a fire(0058).

Overheated engines, or fire(0059).

Most engines are water-cooled(0060). If your engine overheats, you should wait until it has cooled naturally(0061). Only then remove the coolant filler cap, and add water, or other coolant(0062).

If your vehicle catches fire, get the occupants out of the vehicle quickly, and to a safe place(0063). Do not attempt to extinguish a fire in the engine compartment, as opening the bonnet will make the fire flare(0064). Call the fire brigade(0065).

 

b0006

Petrol stations-fuel tank-fuel leaks(0066).

Ensure that, when filling up your vehicles tank, or any fuel cans you are carrying, you do not spill fuel on the forecourt(0067). Any spilled fuel, should be immediately reported to the petrol station attendant(0068). Diesel spillage is dangerous to other road users, particularly motor-cyclists, as it will significantly reduce the level of grip between the tyres, and road surface(0069). Double-check for fuel leaks, and make sure that(0070).

·         you do not overfill your fuel tank(0071).

·         the fuel cap is fastened securely(0072).

·         the seal in the cap is not torn, perished, or missing(0073).

·         there is no visual damage to the cap, or the fuel tank(0074).

Emergency fuel caps, if fitted, should form a good seal(0075).

Never smoke, or use a mobile phone, on the forecourt of petrol stations, as these are major fire risks, and could cause an explosion(0076).

 

b0007

Vehicle security(0077).

When you leave your vehicle you should(0078).

·         remove the ignition key, and engage the steering lock(0079).

·         lock the car, even if you only leave it for a few minutes(0080).

·         close the windows completely(0081).

·         never leave children, or pets in an unventilated car(0082).

·         take all contents with you, or lock them in the boot(0083). Remember, for all a thief knows a carrier bag may contain valuables(0084).

·         never leave vehicle documents in the car(0085).

For extra security, fit an anti-theft device such as an alarm, or immobiliser(0086). If you are buying a new car, it is a good idea to check the level of built-in security features(0087). Consider having your registration number etched on all your car windows(0088). This is a cheap, and effective deterrent to professional thieves(0089).
