Waiting, and parking (238-252)---(0001).

238, (0002).

You MUST NOT wait, or park on yellow lines, during the times of operation, shown on nearby time plates (or zone entry signs, if in a Controlled Parking Zone, that has no local signs, only signs at the edge of the zone) - see Information signs, and Road markings, such as yellow lines, Restricted Parking Bays or No Lines, No Restrictions, etc(0003). Controlled Parking Zone times, normally the same as paid, or resident parking(0090). Double yellow lines, indicate a prohibition of waiting, at any time, even if there are no upright signs(0004). You MUST NOT wait, or park, or stop to set down, and pick up passengers, on school entrance markings (see Road markings) when upright signs indicate a prohibition of stopping(0005).

Law (0006).

 

You MAY load in Double, and active Single yellow lines, for 20 minutes, if no pavement markings or signs (see below)(0091).

DO NOT park on an inactive yellow line if causing an obstruction, especially on narrow roads and opposite other cars(0092).

You MAY park in a cycle lane, if Broken Line and no other restrictions, or Solid Line, and outside restricted times, (see above rule 140)(0093).

Parking found, check list (0007).

239, (0008).

Use off-street parking areas, or bays marked out with white lines, on the road, as parking places, wherever possible(0009).

If you have to stop on the roadside(0010).

·       do not park facing against the traffic flow(0011).

·       stop as close as you can to the side(0012).

·       do not stop too close to a vehicle displaying a Blue Badge: remember, the occupant may need more room to get in, or out(0013).

·       you MUST switch off the engine, headlights, and fog lights(0014).

·       you MUST apply the handbrake before leaving the vehicle(0015).

·       you MUST ensure you do not hit anyone, when you open your door, especially on busy and narrow roads (0016). Check for cyclists, or other traffic, check mirror, and open door slowly (see drawing)(0017).

·       it is safer for your passengers (especially children) to get out of the vehicle on the side next to the kerb(0018).

·       put all valuables out of sight, and make sure your vehicle is secure(0019).

·       lock your vehicle(0020).

Law (0021).

240, (0022).

You MUST NOT stop, or park on(0023).

·       the carriageway, or the hard shoulder, of a motorway, except in an emergency (see Rule 270)(0024).

·       a pedestrian crossing, including the area marked by the zig-zag lines (see Rule 191)(0025).

·       a clearway (see Traffic signs)(0026).

·       taxi bays, as indicated by upright signs, and markings(0027).

·       an urban clearway, within its hours of operation, even when a broken white line is on your side of the road, except to pick up, or set down passengers (see Traffic signs)(0028).

·       a road marked with double white lines, except to pick up, or set down passengers(0029).

·       a tram, or cycle lane during its period of operation(0030).

·       a cycle track(0031).

·       red lines, in the case of specially designated red routes, unless otherwise indicated by signs(0032).

Any vehicle may enter a bus lane to stop, load, or unload where this is not prohibited (see Rule 141)(0033).

Law (0034).

244, (0055).

You MUST NOT park partially, or wholly on the pavement in London, and should not do so elsewhere, unless signs permit it(0056). Parking on the pavement, can obstruct, and seriously inconvenience pedestrians, people in wheelchairs, or with visual impairments, and people with prams, or pushchairs(0057).

Law (0058).

Controlled Parking Zones, 245(0059).

The zone entry signs indicate the times when the waiting restrictions within the zone are in force(0060). Parking may be allowed in some places at other times(0061). Otherwise parking will be within separately signed, and marked bays(0062).

Goods vehicles, 246(0063).

Vehicles with a maximum laden weight of over 7.5 tonnes (including any trailer) MUST NOT be parked on a verge, pavement, or any land situated between carriageways, without police permission(0064). The only exception is when parking is essential for loading, and unloading, in which case the vehicle MUST NOT be left unattended(0065).

Law (0066).

 

b0007

Loading and unloading, 247(0067).

Do not load, or unload, where there are yellow markings on the kerb, and upright signs advise restrictions are in place (see Road markings)(0068). This may be permitted where parking is otherwise restricted(0069). On red routes, specially marked, and signed bays indicate where, and when loading, and unloading is permitted(0070).

Law (0071).

Parking at night(0072).

248, (0073).

You MUST NOT park on a road at night, facing against the direction of the traffic flow, unless in a recognised parking space(0074).

Law (0075).

249, (0076).

All vehicles MUST display parking lights, when parked on a road, or a lay-by, on a road with a speed limit greater than 30 mph (48 km-h)(0077).

Law (0078).

 

b0008

250, (0079).

Cars, goods vehicles, not exceeding 1525 kg unladen weight, invalid carriages, motorcycles, and pedal cycles, may be parked without lights on a road (or lay-by) with a speed limit of 30 mph (48 km-h), or less if they are(0080).

·       at least 10 metres (32 feet) away from any junction, close to the kerb, and facing in the direction of the traffic flow(0081).

·       in a recognised parking place, or lay-by(0082).

Other vehicles, and trailers, and all vehicles with projecting loads, MUST NOT be left on a road at night, without lights(0083).

Law (0084).

Parking in fog, 251(0085).

It is especially dangerous to park on the road in fog(0086). If it is unavoidable, leave your parking lights or sidelights on(0087).
