Breakdowns, and incidents (274-287)----(0001).

Breakdowns(0002).274, (0003).

If your vehicle breaks down, think first of all other road users and(0004).

·         get your vehicle off the road if possible(0005).

·         warn other traffic, by using your hazard warning lights, if your vehicle is causing an obstruction(0006).

·         help other road users see you, by wearing light-coloured, or fluorescent clothing in daylight, and reflective clothing at night, or in poor visibility(0007).

·         put a warning triangle on the road at least 45 metres (147 feet) behind your broken-down vehicle, on the same side of the road, or use other permitted warning devices if you have them(0008). Always take great care when placing, or retrieving them, but never use them on motorways(0009).

·         if possible, keep your sidelights on if it is dark, or visibility is poor(0010).

·         do not stand (or let anybody else stand) between your vehicle, and oncoming traffic(0011).

·         at night, or in poor visibility, do not stand where you will prevent other road users seeing your lights(0012).

Additional rules for the motorway(0013).

275, (0014).

If your vehicle develops a problem, leave the motorway at the next exit, or pull into a service area(0015).

If you cannot do so, you should(0016).

·         pull on to the hard shoulder, and stop as far to the left as possible, with your wheels turned to the left(0017).

·         try to stop near an emergency telephone (situated at approximately one-mile intervals along the hard shoulder)(0018).

·         leave the vehicle by the left-hand door, and ensure your passengers do the same(0019). You MUST leave any animals in the vehicle or, in an emergency, keep them under proper control on the verge(0020). Never attempt to place a warning triangle on a motorway(0021).

276, (0032).

Before you rejoin the carriageway after a breakdown, build up speed on the hard shoulder, and watch for a safe gap in the traffic(0033). Be aware that other vehicles may be stationary on the hard shoulder(0034).

277, (0035).

If you cannot get your vehicle onto the hard shoulder(0036).

·         do not attempt to place any warning device on the carriageway(0037).

·         switch on your hazard warning lights(0038).

·         leave your vehicle, only when you can safely get clear, of the carriageway(0039).

Disabled drivers, 278(0040).

If you have a disability which prevents you from following the above advice you should(0041).

·         stay in your vehicle(0042).

·         switch on your hazard warning lights(0043).

·         display a Help pennant or, if you have a car, or mobile telephone, contact the emergency services, and be prepared to advise them of your location(0044).

 

b0005

Obstructions(0045).

279, (0046).

If anything falls from your vehicle (or any other vehicle) on to the road, stop, and retrieve it, only if it is safe to do so(0047).

Motorways, 280(0048).

On a motorway do not try to remove the obstruction yourself(0049). Stop at the next emergency telephone, and call the Highways Agency, or the police(0050).

Incidents(0051).

Warning signs, or flashing lights, 281(0052).

If you see, or hear emergency, or incident support vehicles in the distance, be aware there may be an incident ahead (see Rule 219)(0053). Police Officers, and Highways Agency Traffic Officers may be required to work in the carriageway, for example dealing with debris, collisions, or conducting rolling road blocks(0054). Police officers will use rear-facing flashing red, and blue lights, and HA Traffic Officers will use rear-facing flashing red, and amber lights, in these situations(0055). Watch out for such signals, slow down, and be prepared to stop(0056). You MUST follow any directions given by Police officers, or Traffic officers as to whether you can safely pass the incident, or blockage(0057).

Law (0058).

282, (0059).

When passing the scene of an incident, or crash do not be distracted, or slow down unnecessarily (for example if an incident is on the other side of a dual carriageway)(0060). This may cause a collision, or traffic congestion, but see Rule 283(0061).

b0007

If you are involved in a crash, or stop to give assistance(0062).

·         use your hazard warning lights to warn other traffic(0063).

·         ask drivers to switch off their engines, and stop smoking(0064).

·         arrange for the emergency services to be called immediately, with full details of the incident location, and any casualties (on a motorway, use the emergency telephone which allows easy location by the emergency services(0065). If you use a mobile phone, first make sure you have identified your location from the marker posts, on the side of the hard shoulder)(0066).

·         move uninjured people away from the vehicles to safety; on a motorway this should, if possible, be well away from the traffic, the hard shoulder, and the central reservation(0067).

·         do not move injured people from their vehicles unless they are in immediate danger from fire, or explosion(0068).

·         do not remove a motor-cyclists helmet unless it is essential to do so(0069).

·         be prepared to give first aid, as shown in First aid on the road(0070).

·         stay at the scene until emergency services arrive(0071).

If you are involved in any other medical emergency on the motorway, you should contact the emergency services in the same way(0072).

 

b0008

Incidents involving dangerous goods(0073).

284, (0074).

Vehicles carrying dangerous goods in packages, will be marked with plain orange reflective plates(0075). Road tankers, and vehicles carrying tank containers of dangerous goods, will have hazard warning plates (see Vehicle markings)(0076).

285, (0077).

If an incident involves a vehicle containing dangerous goods, follow the advice in Rule 283 and, in particular(0078).

·         switch off engines, and DO NOT SMOKE(0079).

·         keep well away from the vehicle, and do not be tempted to try to rescue casualties, as you yourself could become one(0080).

·         call the emergency services, and give as much information as possible about the labels, and markings on the vehicle(0081). DO NOT use a mobile phone close to a vehicle carrying flammable loads(0082).

Documentation, 286(0083).

If you are involved in a collision which causes damage, or injury to any other person, vehicle, animal, or property, you MUST(0084).

·         stop(0085).

·         give your own, and the vehicle owners name, and address, and the registration number of the vehicle, to anyone having reasonable grounds for requiring them(0086).

·         if you do not give your name, and address at the time of the collision, report it to the police as soon as reasonably practicable, and in any case within 24 hours(0087).

Law (0088).

287, (0089).

If another person is injured, and you do not produce your insurance certificate, at the time of the crash, to a police officer, or to anyone having reasonable grounds to request it, you MUST(0090).

·         report it to the police as soon as possible, and in any case within 24 hours(0091).

·         produce your insurance certificate, for the police within seven days(0092).

Law (0093).
