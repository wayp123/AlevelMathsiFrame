Motorways (253-273)----(0001).

Many other Rules apply to motorway driving, either wholly, or in part: Rules 46, 57, 83-126, 130-134, 139, 144, 146-151, 160, 161, 219, 221-222, 225, 226-237, 274-278, 280, and 281-290(0002).

General(0003).

Prohibited vehicles, 253(0004).

Motorways MUST NOT be used by pedestrians, holders of provisional motorcycle, or car licences, riders of motorcycles under 50cc, cyclists, horse riders, certain slow-moving vehicles, and those carrying oversized loads (except by special permission), agricultural vehicles, and powered wheelchairs-powered mobility scooters (see Rules 36-46 incl)(0005).

Law (0006).

254, (0007).

Traffic on motorways usually travels faster than on other roads, so you have less time to react(0008). It is especially important to use your mirrors earlier, and look much further ahead, than you would on other roads(0009).

 

b0002

Motorway signals, 255(0010).

Motorway signals (see Light signals controlling traffic) are used to warn you of a danger ahead(0011). For example, there may be an incident, fog, a spillage, or road workers on the carriageway, which you may not immediately be able to see(0012).

256, (0013).

Signals situated on the central reservation apply to all lanes(0014). On very busy stretches, signals may be overhead, with a separate signal for each lane (see drawing)(0015).

Amber flashing lights, 257(0016).

These warn of a hazard ahead(0017). The signal may show a temporary maximum speed limit, lanes that are closed, or a message such as Fog(0018). Adjust your speed, and look out for the danger, until you pass a signal, which is not flashing, or one that gives the All clear sign, and you are sure it is safe to increase your speed(0019).

Red flashing lights, 258(0020).

If red lights on the overhead signals flash above your lane, and a red X' is showing, you MUST NOT go beyond the signal in that lane(0021). If red lights flash on a signal in the central reservation, or at the side of the road, you MUST NOT go beyond the signal in any lane(0022).

Law (0023).

 

b0003

Driving on the motorway(0024).

Joining the motorway, 259(0025).

When you join the motorway, you will normally approach it from a road on the left (a slip road), or from an adjoining motorway(0026).

You should(0027).

·         give priority to traffic already on the motorway(0028).

·         check the traffic on the motorway, and match your speed to fit safely into the traffic flow in the left-hand lane(0029).

·         not cross solid white lines that separate lanes, or use the hard shoulder(0030).

·         stay on the slip road, if it continues as an extra lane on the motorway(0031).

·         remain in the left-hand lane long enough, to adjust to the speed of traffic, before considering overtaking(0032).

 

b0004

On the motorway(0033).

260, (0034).

When you can see well ahead, and the road conditions are good, you should(0035).

·         drive at a steady cruising speed which you, and your vehicle can handle safely, and is within the speed limit (see Rule 124)(0036).

·         keep a safe distance from the vehicle in front, and increase the gap on wet, or icy roads, or in fog (see Rules 126, and 235)(0037).

261, (0038).

You MUST NOT exceed 70 mph (112 km-h), or the maximum speed limit permitted for your vehicle (see Rule 124)(0039). If a lower speed limit is in force, either permanently, or temporarily, at road works for example, you MUST NOT exceed the lower limit(0040). On some motorways, mandatory motorway signals (which display the speed within a red ring) are used to vary the maximum speed limit, to improve traffic flow(0041). You MUST NOT exceed this speed limit(0042).

Law (0043).

 

b0005

262, (0044).

The monotony of driving on a motorway can make you feel sleepy(0045). To minimise the risk, follow the advice in Rule 91(0046).

263, (0047).

You MUST NOT reverse, cross the central reservation, or drive against the traffic flow(0048). If you have missed your exit, or have taken the wrong route, carry on to the next exit(0049).

Law (0050).

Lane discipline(0051).

264, (0052).

You should always drive in the left-hand lane, when the road ahead is clear(0053). If you are overtaking a number of slower-moving vehicles, you should return to the left-hand lane, as soon as you are safely past(0054). Slow-moving, or speed-restricted vehicles, should always remain in the left-hand lane of the carriageway, unless overtaking(0055). You MUST NOT drive on the hard shoulder, except in an emergency, or if directed to do so by the police, HA traffic officers in uniform, or by signs(0056).

Law (0057).

 

b0006

265, (0058).

The right-hand lane of a motorway with three, or more lanes MUST NOT be used (except in prescribed circumstances) if you are driving(0059).

·         any vehicle drawing a trailer(0060).

·         a goods vehicle with a maximum laden weight exceeding 3.5 tonnes but not exceeding 7.5 tonnes, which is required to be fitted with a speed limiter(0061).

·         a goods vehicle with a maximum laden weight exceeding 7.5 tonnes(0062).

·         a passenger vehicle with a maximum laden weight exceeding 7.5 tonnes constructed, or adapted to carry more than eight seated passengers in addition to the driver(0063).

·         a passenger vehicle with a maximum laden weight not exceeding 7.5 tonnes which is constructed, or adapted to carry more than eight seated passengers, in addition to the driver, which is required to be fitted with a speed limiter(0064).

Law (0065).

 

b0007

Approaching a junction, 266(0066).

Look well ahead for signals, or signs(0067). Direction signs may be placed over the road(0068). If you need to change lanes, do so in good time(0069). At some junctions, a lane may lead directly off the motorway(0070). Only get in that lane, if you wish to go in the direction, indicated on the overhead signs(0071).

 

b0008

Overtaking(0072).

267, (0073).

Do not overtake unless you are sure it is safe, and legal to do so(0074). Overtake only on the right(0075). You should(0076).

·         check your mirrors(0077).

·         take time to judge the speeds correctly(0078).

·         make sure that the lane you will be joining, is sufficiently clear ahead, and behind(0079).

·         take a quick sideways glance into the blind spot area, to verify the position of a vehicle, that may have disappeared from your view, in the mirror(0080).

·         remember that traffic may be coming up behind you very quickly(0081). Check all your mirrors carefully(0082). Look out for motor-cyclists(0083). When it is safe to do so, signal in plenty of time, then move out(0084).

·         ensure you do not cut in on the vehicle you have overtaken(0085).

·         be especially careful at night, and in poor visibility, when it is harder to judge speed, and distance(0086).

 

b0009

268, (0087).

Do not overtake on the left, or move to a lane on your left to overtake(0088). In congested conditions, where adjacent lanes of traffic are moving at similar speeds, traffic in left-hand lanes may sometimes be moving faster than traffic to the right(0089). In these conditions you may keep up with the traffic in your lane, even if this means passing traffic, in the lane to your right(0090). Do not weave in, and out of lanes to overtake(0091).

Hard shoulder, 269(0092).

You MUST NOT use the hard shoulder for overtaking(0093). In areas where an Active Traffic Management (ATM) Scheme is in force, the hard shoulder may be used as a running lane(0094). You will know when you can use, this because a speed limit sign will be shown above all open lanes, including the hard shoulder(0095). A red cross, or blank sign, above the hard shoulder, means that you MUST NOT drive on the hard shoulder, except in an emergency, or breakdown(0096). Emergency refuge areas, have also been built into these areas, for use in cases of emergency, or breakdown(0097).

Law (0098).

 

b0010

Stopping(0099).

270, (0100).

You MUST NOT stop on the carriageway, hard shoulder, slip road, central reservation, or verge except in an emergency, or when told to do so by the police, HA traffic officers in uniform, an emergency sign, or by flashing red light signals(0101). Do not stop on the hard shoulder, to either make or receive mobile phone calls(0102).

Law (0103).

271, (0104).

You MUST NOT pick up, or set down anyone, or walk on a motorway, except in an emergency(0105).

Law (0106).

 

b0011

Leaving the motorway(0107).

272, (0108).

Unless signs indicate, that a lane leads directly off the motorway, you will normally leave the motorway by a slip road on your left(0109).

You should(0110).

·         watch for the signs letting you know you are getting near your exit(0111).

·         move into the left-hand lane well before reaching your exit(0112).

·         signal left in good time, and reduce your speed on the slip road as necessary(0113).

273, (0114).

On leaving the motorway, or using a link road between motorways, your speed may be higher than you realise - 50 mph may feel like 30 mph(0115). Check your speedometer, and adjust your speed accordingly(0116). Some slip-roads, and link roads have sharp bends, so you will need to slow down(0117).
