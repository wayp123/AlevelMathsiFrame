Road users requiring extra care (204-225)(0001).

204, (0002).

The most vulnerable road users are pedestrians, cyclists, motor-cyclists, and horse riders(0003). It is particularly important, to be aware of children, older, and disabled people, learner, and inexperienced drivers, and riders(0004).

Pedestrians(0005).

205, (0006).

There is a risk of pedestrians, especially children, stepping unexpectedly into the road(0007). You should drive with the safety of children in mind, at a speed suitable for the conditions(0008).

206, (0009).

 

b0002

Drive carefully, and slowly when(0010).

·         in crowded shopping streets, Home Zones, and Quiet Lanes (see Rule 218), or residential areas(0011).

·         driving past bus, and tram stops; pedestrians may emerge suddenly into the road(0012).

·         passing parked vehicles, especially ice cream vans; children are more interested in ice cream than traffic, and may run into the road unexpectedly (see drawing)(0013).

·         needing to cross a pavement, or cycle track; for example, to reach, or leave a driveway(0014). Give way to pedestrians, and cyclists on the pavement(0015).

·         reversing into a side road; look all around the vehicle, and give way to any pedestrians who may be crossing the road(0016).

·         turning at road junctions; give way to pedestrians, who are already crossing the road, into which you are turning(0017).

·         the pavement is closed due to street repairs, and pedestrians are directed to use the road(0018).

·         approaching pedestrians on narrow rural roads without a footway, or footpath(0019). Always slow down, and be prepared to stop if necessary, giving them plenty of room, as you drive past(0020).

 

b0003

Particularly vulnerable pedestrians, 207(0021).

These include(0022).

·         children, and older pedestrians, who may not be able to judge your speed, and could step into the road in front of you(0023). At 40 mph (64 km-h) your vehicle will probably kill any pedestrians it hits(0024). At 20 mph (32 km-h) there is only a 1 in 20 chance of the pedestrian being killed(0025). So kill your speed(0026).

·         older pedestrians, who may need more time to cross the road(0027). Be patient, and allow them to cross, in their own time(0028). Do not hurry them, by revving your engine, or edging forward(0029).

·         people with disabilities(0030). People with hearing impairments, may not be aware of your vehicle approaching(0031). Those with walking difficulties, require more time(0032).

·         blind, or partially sighted people, who may be carrying a white cane, or using a guide dog(0033). They may not be able to see you approaching(0034).

·         deaf-blind people, who may be carrying a white cane with a red band, or using a dog with a red, and white harness(0035). They may not see, or hear instructions, or signals(0036).

 

b0004

Near schools, 208(0037).

Drive slowly, and be particularly aware of young cyclists, and pedestrians(0038). In some places, there may be a flashing amber signal, below the School warning sign, which tells you, that there may be children crossing the road ahead(0039). Drive very slowly, until you are clear, of the area(0040).

209, (0041).

Drive carefully, and slowly, when passing a stationary bus, showing a School Bus sign (see Vehicle markings) as children may be getting on, or off(0042).

210, (0043).

You MUST stop, when a school crossing patrol shows, a Stop for children sign (see Signals by authorised persons, and Signs giving orders)(0044).

Law (0045).
