Pedestrian crossings (191-199)(0001). (9980).

191, (0002).

You MUST NOT park on a crossing, or in the area covered by the zig-zag lines(0003). You MUST NOT overtake the moving vehicle nearest the crossing, or the vehicle nearest the crossing, which has stopped, to give way to pedestrians(0004).

Law (0005).

192, (0006).

In queuing traffic, you should keep the crossing clear (see diagram) (0007).

193, (0008).

You should take extra care, where the view of either side of the crossing, is blocked, by queuing traffic, or incorrectly parked vehicles(0009). Pedestrians may be crossing between stationary vehicles(0010).

194, (0011).

Allow pedestrians plenty of time to cross, and do not harass them, by revving your engine, or edging forward(0012).

 

b0002

Zebra crossings, 195(0013).

As you approach a zebra crossing(0014).

·         look out for pedestrians waiting to cross, and be ready to slow down, or stop, to let them cross(0015).

·         you MUST give way, when a pedestrian has moved onto a crossing(0016).

·         allow more time for, stopping on wet, or icy roads(0017).

·         do not wave, or use your horn, to invite pedestrians across; this could be dangerous, if another vehicle is approaching(0018).

·         be aware of pedestrians, approaching from the side, of the crossing(0019).

A zebra crossing, with a central island, is two separate crossings (see Rule 20)(0020).

Law (0021).
