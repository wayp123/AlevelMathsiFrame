Road junctions (170-183)---(0001).

170, (0002).

Take extra care at junctions(0003). You should_(0004).

·         watch out for cyclists, motor-cyclists, powered wheelchairs, mobility scooters, and pedestrians, as they are not always easy to see(0005). Be aware that they may not have seen, or heard you, if you are approaching from behind(0006).

·         watch out for pedestrians crossing a road into which you are turning(0007). If they have started to cross, they have priority, so give way {see drawing}(0008).

·         watch out for long vehicles, which may be turning at a junction ahead; they may have to use the whole width of the road, to make the turn {see Rule 221}(0009).

·         watch out for horse riders, who may take a different line on the road, from that which you would expect(0010).

·         not assume, when waiting at a junction, that a vehicle coming from the right, and signalling left will actually turn(0011). Wait, and make sure(0012).

·         look all around before emerging(0013). Do not cross, or join a road, until there is a gap large enough for you to do so safely {a larger gap, is needed to join a road, than to cross a road} (0014).

171, (0015).

You MUST stop behind the line, at a junction with a Stop sign, and a solid white line across the road(0016). Wait for a safe gap in the traffic, before you move off(0017).

Law (0018).

172, (0019).

The approach to a junction, may have a Give Way sign, or a triangle marked on the road(0020). You MUST give way to traffic, on the main road, when emerging from a junction, with broken white lines across the road(0021). Slow down, and stop, if neccessary(0023).
