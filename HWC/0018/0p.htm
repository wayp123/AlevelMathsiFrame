Signals (103-112)---(0001).

103, (0002).

Signals warn, and inform other road users, including pedestrians (see Signals to other road users), of your intended actions(0003). You should always(0004).

·         give clear signals in plenty of time, having checked, it is not misleading, to signal at that time(0005).

·         use them to advise other road users, before changing course, or direction, stopping, or moving off(0006).

·         cancel them after use(0007).

·         make sure your signals will not confuse others(0008). If, for instance, you want to stop after a side road, do not signal until you are passing the road(0009). If you signal earlier, it may give the impression, that you intend to turn into the road(0010). Your brake lights will help warn traffic behind you, that you are slowing down(0011).

·         use an arm signal to emphasise, or reinforce your signal if necessary(0012). Remember that signalling does not give you priority(0013).

104, (0014).

You should also(0015).

·         watch out for signals given by other road users, and proceed only when you are satisfied that it is safe(0016).

·         be aware that an indicator on another vehicle, may not have been cancelled(0017).

105, (0018).

You MUST obey signals given by, police officers, traffic officers, traffic wardens (see Signals by authorised persons), and signs used by school crossing patrols(0019).

b0003

Police stopping procedures, 160(0021).

If the police want to stop your vehicle they will, where possible, attract your attention by(0022).

·         flashing blue lights, headlights, or sounding their siren, or horn, usually from behind(0023).

·         directing you to pull over to the side, by pointing and, or using the left indicator(0024).

You MUST then pull over, and stop, as soon as it is safe to do so(0025). Then switch off your engine(0026).

Law (0027).

 

b0004

Other stopping procedures(0028).

107, (0029).

Vehicle & Operator Services Agency Officers, have powers to stop vehicles on all roads, including motorways, and trunk roads, in England, and Wales(0030). They will attract your attention by flashing amber lights(0031).

·         either from the front, requesting you to follow them, to a safe place to stop(0032).

·         or from behind, directing you to pull over, to the side by pointing and, or using the left indicator(0033).

It is an offence not to comply with their directions(0034). You MUST obey any signals given (see Signals by authorised persons)(0035).

Law (0036).

 

b0005

108, (0037).

Highways Agency Traffic Officers, have powers to stop vehicles on most motorways, and some A' class roads, in England only(0038). If  Highways Agency traffic officers in uniform, want to stop your vehicle on safety grounds (e g, an insecure load) they will, where possible, attract your attention by(0039).

·         flashing amber lights, usually from behind(0040).

·         directing you to pull over to the side, by pointing and, or using the left indicator(0041).

You MUST then pull over, and stop, as soon as it is safe to do so(0042). Then switch off your engine(0043). It is an offence, not to comply with their directions (see Signals by authorised persons)(0044).

Law (0045).

Traffic light signals, and traffic signs, 109(0046).

You MUST obey all traffic light signals (see Light signals controlling traffic), and traffic signs giving orders, including temporary signals, and signs (see Signs giving orders, Warning signs, Direction signs)(0047). Make sure you know, understand, and act on all other traffic, and information signs, and road markings(0048).

Law (0049).

 

b0006

Flashing headlights, 110(0050).

Only flash your headlights, to let other road users know, that you are there(0051). Do not flash your headlights, to convey any other message, or intimidate other road users(0052).

111, (0053).

Never assume that flashing headlights, is a signal inviting you to proceed(0054). Use your own judgement, and proceed carefully(0055).
