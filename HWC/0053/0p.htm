Road works, level crossings, and tramways (288-307)----(0001).

Road works_(0002).

288, (0003).

When the Road Works Ahead sign is displayed, you will need to be more watchful, and look for additional signs providing more specific instructions(0004). Observe all signs - they are there for your safety, and the safety of road workers(0005).

·         you MUST NOT exceed any temporary maximum speed limit(0006).

·         use your mirrors, and get into the correct lane for your vehicle in good time, and as signs direct(0007).

·         do not switch lanes to overtake queuing traffic(0008).

·         take extra care near cyclists, and motor-cyclists as they are vulnerable to skidding on grit, mud, or other debris at road works(0009).

·         where lanes are restricted due to road works, merge in turn {see Rule 134}(0010).

b0002

·         do not drive through an area marked off by traffic cones(0011).

·         watch out for traffic entering, or leaving the works area, but do not be distracted by what is going on there(0012). Concentrate on the road ahead, not the road works(0013).

·         bear in mind that the road ahead, may be obstructed by the works, or by slow moving, or stationary traffic(0014).

·         keep a safe distance - there could be queues in front(0015).

To obtain further information about road works, see Other information(0016).

Law (0017).

 

b0003

Additional rules for high-speed roads(0018).

289, (0019).

Take special care on motorways, and other high-speed dual carriageways(0020).

·         one, or more lanes may be closed to traffic, and a lower speed limit may apply(0021).

·         works vehicles that are slow moving, or stationary with a large Keep Left, or Keep Right sign on the back are sometimes used, to close lanes for repairs, and a flashing light arrow may also be used to make the works vehicle more conspicuous from a distance, and give earlier warning to drivers, that they need to move over to the next lane(0022).

·         check mirrors, slow down, and change lanes if necessary(0023).

·         keep a safe distance from the vehicle in front {see Rule 126}(0024).

 

b0004

290, (0025).

Contraflow systems mean, that you may be travelling in a narrower lane than normal, and with no permanent barrier between you, and oncoming traffic(0026). The hard shoulder may be used for traffic, but be aware that there may be broken-down vehicles ahead of you(0027). Keep a good distance from the vehicle ahead, and observe any temporary speed limits(0028).

Level crossings(0029).

291, (0030).

A level crossing is where a road crosses a railway, or tramway line(0031). Approach, and cross it with care(0032). Never drive onto a crossing, until the road is clear on the other side, and do not get too close to the car in front(0033). Never stop, or park on, or near, a crossing(0034).

 

b0005

Overhead electric lines, 292(0035).

It is dangerous to touch overhead electric lines(0036). You MUST obey the safe height warning road signs, and you should not continue forward onto the railway, if your vehicle touches any height barrier, or bells(0037). The clearance available is usually 5 metres {16 feet 6 inches} but may be lower(0038).

Law (0039).

 

b0006

Controlled crossings, 293(0040).

Most crossings have traffic light signals, with a steady amber light, twin flashing red stop lights {see Light signals controlling traffic, and Warning signs}, and an audible alarm for pedestrians(0041). They may have full, half, or no barriers(0042).

·         you MUST always obey the flashing red stop lights {see drawing}(0043).

·         you MUST stop behind the white line across the road(0044).

·         keep going, if you have already crossed the white line, when the amber light comes on(0045).

·         do not reverse onto, or over a controlled crossing(0046).

·         you MUST wait if a train goes by, and the red lights continue to flash(0047). This means another train will be passing soon(0048).

·         only cross when the lights go off, and barriers open(0049).

·         never zig-zag around half-barriers, they lower automatically because a train is approaching(0050).

·         at crossings where there are no barriers, a train is approaching when the lights show(0051).

Law (0052).

 

b0007

Railway telephones, 294(0053).

If you are driving a large, or slow-moving vehicle, a long low vehicle with a risk of grounding, or if you are herding animals, a train could arrive before you are clear of the crossing(0054). You MUST obey any sign instructing you to use the railway telephone, to obtain permission to cross(0055). You MUST also telephone when clear of the crossing, if requested to do so(0056).

Law (0057).

Crossings without traffic lights, 295(0058).

Vehicles should stop, and wait at the barrier, or gate when it begins to close, and not cross until the barrier, or gate opens(0059).

User-operated gates, or barriers, 296(0060).

Some crossings have Stop signs, and small red, and green lights(0061). You MUST NOT cross when the red light is showing, only cross if the green light is on(0062). If crossing with a vehicle, you should(0063).

·         open the gates, or barriers on both sides of the crossing(0064).

·         check that the green light is still on, and cross quickly(0065).

·         close the gates, or barriers when you are clear of the crossing(0066).

Law (0067).

 

b0008

297, (0068).

If there are no lights, follow the procedure in Rule 295(0069). Stop, look both ways, and listen before you cross(0070). If there is a railway telephone, always use it to contact the signal operator to make sure it is safe to cross(0071). Inform the signal operator again when you are clear of the crossing(0072).

Open crossings, 298(0073).

These have no gates, barriers, attendant, or traffic lights, but will have a Give Way sign(0074). You should look both ways, listen, and make sure there is no train coming before you cross(0075).

Incidents, and breakdowns, 299(0076).

If your vehicle breaks down, or if you have an incident, on a crossing you should(0077).

·         get everyone out of the vehicle, and clear of the crossing immediately(0078).

·         use a railway telephone, if available, to tell the signal operator(0079). Follow the instructions you are given(0080).

·         move the vehicle clear of the crossing, if there is time, before a train arrives(0081). If the alarm sounds, or the amber light comes on, leave the vehicle, and get clear of the crossing immediately(0082).

 

b0009

Tramways(0083).

300, (0084).

You MUST NOT enter a road, lane, or other route reserved for trams(0085). Take extra care where, trams run along the road(0086). You should avoid driving directly on top of the rails, and should take care where trams leave the main carriageway, to enter the reserved route, to ensure you do not follow them(0087). The width taken up by trams, is often shown by tram lanes, marked by, white lines, yellow dots, or by a different type of road surface(0088). Diamond-shaped signs, and white light signals, give instructions to tram drivers only(0089).

Law (0090).

301, (0091).

Take extra care where, the track crosses from one side of the road to the other, and where the road narrows, and the tracks come close to the kerb(0092). Tram drivers usually have their own traffic signals, and may be permitted to move when you are not(0093). Always give way to trams(0094). Do not try to race, or overtake them, or pass them on the inside, unless they are at tram stops, or stopped by tram signals, and there is a designated tram lane for you to pass(0095).

 

b0010

302, (0096).

You MUST NOT park your vehicle where it would get in the way of trams, or where it would force other drivers to do so(0097). Do not stop on any part of a tram track, except in a designated bay where this has been provided alongside, and clear of the track(0098). When doing so, ensure that all parts of your vehicle are outside the delineated tram path(0099). Remember that a tram cannot steer round an obstruction(0100).

Law (0101).

Tram stops, 303(0102).

Where the tram stops at a platform, either in the middle, or at the side of the road, you MUST follow the route shown by the road signs, and markings(0103). At stops without platforms, you MUST NOT drive between a tram, and the left-hand kerb, when a tram has stopped to pick up passengers(0104). If there is no alternative route signed, do not overtake the tram - wait until it moves off(0105).

Law (0106).

 

b0011

304, (0107).

Look out for pedestrians, especially children, running to catch a tram approaching a stop(0108).

305, (0109).

Always give priority to trams, especially when they signal to pull away from stops, unless it would be unsafe to do so(0110). Remember that they may be carrying large numbers of standing passengers, who could be injured, if the tram had to make an emergency stop(0111). Look out for people getting off a bus, or tram, and crossing the road(0112).

306, (0113).

All road users, but particularly cyclists, and motor-cyclists, should take extra care when driving, or riding close to, or crossing the tracks, especially if the rails are wet(0114). You should take particular care when crossing the rails at shallow angles, on bends, and at junctions(0115). It is safest to cross the tracks directly at right angles(0116). Other road users should be aware that cyclists, and motor-cyclists may need more space to cross the tracks safely(0117).

 

b0012

Overhead electric lines, 307(0118).

Tramway overhead wires are normally 5.8 metres above any carriageway, but can be lower(0119). You should ensure that you have sufficient clearance, between the wire, and your vehicle {including any load you are carrying} before driving under an overhead wire(0120). Drivers of vehicles with extending cranes, booms, tipping apparatus, or other types of variable height equipment, should ensure that the equipment is fully lowered(0121). Where overhead wires are set lower than 5.8 metres, these will be indicated by height clearance markings - similar to low bridge signs(0122). The height clearances on these plates should be carefully noted, and observed(0123). If you are in any doubt as to whether your vehicle will pass safely under the wires, you should always contact the local police, or the tramway operator(0124). Never take a chance as this can be extremely hazardous(0125).
