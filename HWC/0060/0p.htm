Penalties(0001).

Parliament, sets the maximum penalties, for road traffic offences(0002). The seriousness of the offence, is reflected in the maximum penalty(0003). It is for the courts to decide, what sentence to impose, according to circumstances(0004).

The penalty table, indicates some of the main offences, and the associated penalties(0005). There is a wide range of other more specific offences which, for the sake of simplicity, are not shown here(0006). The penalty points, and disqualification system is described below(0007).

 

b0002

Penalty points, and disqualification(0008).

The penalty point system, is intended to deter drivers, and motor-cyclists, from following unsafe motoring practices(0009). Certain non-motoring offences, e g, failure to rectify vehicle defects, can also attract penalty points(0010). The court MUST order points to be endorsed on the licence, according to the fixed number, or the range set by Parliament(0011). The accumulation of penalty points, acts as a warning to drivers, and motor-cyclists, that they risk disqualification, if further offences are committed(0012).

Law (0013).

A driver, or motor-cyclist, who accumulates 12, or more penalty points, within a three-year period MUST be disqualified(0014). This will be for a minimum period of six months, or longer if the driver, or motorcyclist, has previously been disqualified(0015).

Law (0016).

For every offence which carries penalty points, the court has a discretionary power, to order the licence holder, to be disqualified(0017). This may be for any period the court thinks fit, but will usually be between a week, and a few months(0018).

 

b0003

In the case of serious offences, such as dangerous driving, and drink-driving, the court MUST order disqualification(0019). The minimum period is 12 months, but for repeat offenders, or where the alcohol level is high, it may be longer(0020). For example, a second drink-drive offence, in the space of 10 years, will result in a minimum of three years disqualification(0021).

Law (0022).

Please note, the penalties listed under Imprisonment, Fine, and Disqualification are maximum penalties(0023).

Where a court disqualifies a person on conviction, for one of these offences, it must order an extended retest(0024). The courts also have discretion, to order a retest, for any other offence, which carries penalty points, an extended retest where disqualification is obligatory, and an ordinary test, where disqualification is not obligatory(0025).

Furthermore, in some serious cases, the court MUST (in addition to imposing a fixed period of disqualification) order the offender to be disqualified, until they pass a driving test(0026). In other cases, the court has a discretionary power, to order such disqualification(0027). The test may be an ordinary length test, or an extended test, according to the nature of the offence(0028).

Law (0029).

 

b0004

New drivers(0030).

Special rules, as set out below, apply for a period of two years, from the date of passing their first driving test, to drivers, and motor-cyclists from(0031).

·         the UK, EU-EEA, the Isle of Man, the Channel Islands, or Gibraltar, who passed their first driving test in any of those countries(0032).

·         other foreign countries who have to pass a UK driving test to gain a UK licence, in which case the UK driving test is treated as their first driving test; and (0033).

·         other foreign countries who (without needing a test) exchanged their licence for a UK licence, and subsequently passed a UK driving test, to drive another type of vehicle, in which case the UK driving test is treated as their first driving test(0034). For example, a driver who exchanges a foreign licence (car) for a UK licence (car), and who later passes a test to drive another type of vehicle (e g, an HGV) will be subject to the special rules(0035).

 

b0005

Where a person, subject to the special rules, accumulates six, or more penalty points, before the end of the two-year period (including any points acquired before passing the test) their licence will be revoked automatically(0036). To regain the licence, they must re-apply for a provisional licence, and may drive only as a learner, until they pass a further driving test (see also Safety code for new drivers)(0037).

Law (0038).

Note(0039). This applies even if they pay for offences by fixed penalty(0040). Drivers in the first group (UK, EU-EEA etc,) who already have a full licence, for one type of vehicle, are not affected by the special rules, if they later pass a test to drive another type of vehicle(0041).

 

b0006

Other consequences of offending(0042).

Where an offence is punishable by imprisonment, then the vehicle used to commit the offence, may be confiscated(0043).

Law (0044).

In addition to the penalties, a court may decide to impose, the cost of insurance is likely to rise considerably, following conviction for a serious driving offence(0045). This is because insurance companies, consider such drivers, more likely to be involved in a collision(0046).

Drivers disqualified for drinking, and driving twice within 10 years, or once if they are over two, and a half times the legal limit, or those who refused to give a specimen, also have to satisfy the Driver, and Vehicle Licensing Agencys Medical Branch, that they do not have an alcohol problem, and are otherwise fit to drive, before their licence is returned, at the end of their period of disqualification(0047). Persistent misuse of drugs, or alcohol, may lead to the withdrawal of a driving licence(0048).
