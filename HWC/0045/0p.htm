Motor-cyclists, and cyclists(0001).

211, (0002).

It is often difficult, to see motor-cyclists, and cyclists, especially when, they are coming up from behind, coming out of junctions, at roundabouts, overtaking you, or filtering through traffic(0003). Always look out for them, before you emerge from a junction; they could be approaching faster than you think (see diagram) (0004). When turning right, across a line of slow-moving, or stationary traffic, look out for cyclists, or motor-cyclists, on the inside of the traffic, you are crossing(0005). Be especially careful when turning, and when changing direction, or lane(0006). Be sure to check mirrors, and blind spots carefully(0007).

212, (0008).

When passing motor-cyclists, and cyclists, give them plenty of room (see Rules 162-167)(0009). If they look over their shoulder, it could mean that, they intend to pull out, turn right, or change direction(0010). Give them time, and space to do so(0011).

213, (0012).

Motor-cyclists, and cyclists, may suddenly need to avoid uneven road surfaces, and obstacles such as drain covers, or oily, wet, or icy patches, on the road(0013). Give them plenty of room, and pay particular attention, to any sudden change of direction, they may have to make(0014).

 

b0002

Other road users(0015).

Animals, 214(0016).

When passing animals, drive slowly(0017). Give them plenty of room, and be ready to stop(0018). Do not scare animals, by sounding your horn, revving your engine, or accelerating rapidly, once you have passed them(0019). Look out for, animals being led, driven, or ridden on the road, and take extra care(0020). Keep your speed down at bends, and on narrow country roads(0021). If a road is blocked, by a herd of animals, stop, and switch off your engine, until they have left the road(0022). Watch out for animals, on unfenced roads(0023).

Horse riders, and horse-drawn vehicles, 215(0024).

Be particularly careful of horse riders, and horse-drawn vehicles, especially when overtaking(0025). Always pass wide, and slowly(0026). Horse riders are often children, so take extra care, and remember, riders may ride in double file, when escorting a young, or inexperienced horse, or rider(0027). Look out for horse riders, and horse drivers signals, and heed a request to slow down, or stop(0028). Take great care, and treat all horses, as a potential hazard; they can be unpredictable, despite the efforts of their rider-driver(0029).

 

b0003

Older drivers, 216(0030).

Their reactions may be slower than other drivers(0031). Make allowance for this(0032).

Learners, and inexperienced drivers, 217(0033).

They may not be so skilful at, anticipating, and responding to, events(0034). Be particularly patient with, learner drivers, and young drivers(0035). Drivers who have recently passed their test, may display a new driver plate, or sticker (see Safety code for new drivers)(0036).
