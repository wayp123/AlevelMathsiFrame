Rules for cyclists (59-82)---(0001).

These rules, are in addition to those, that we will see, in the following sections, which apply to all vehicles (except the motorway section)(0002). See also, You and your bicycle(0003).

Clothing, 59(0004).

You should wear(0005).

·         a cycle helmet, which conforms to current regulations, is the correct size, and securely fastened(0006).

·         appropriate clothes for cycling(0007). Avoid clothes which may get tangled in the chain, or in a wheel, or may obscure your lights(0008).

·         light-coloured, or fluorescent clothing, which helps other road users, to see you in daylight, and poor light (see drawing)(0009).

·         reflective clothing, and, or, accessories (belt, arm, or ankle bands) in the dark(0010).

 

 

b0002

At night, 60(0011).

Your cycle MUST have white front, and red rear lights lit(0012). It MUST also be fitted with a red rear reflector (and amber pedal reflectors, if manufactured after 1/10/85)(0013). White front reflectors, and spoke reflectors will also help you to be seen(0014). Flashing lights are permitted, but it is recommended that cyclists, who are riding in areas without street lighting, use a steady front lamp(0015).

Law (0016).

Cycle Routes and Other Facilities, 61(0017).

Use cycle routes, advanced stop lines, cycle boxes, and toucan crossings, unless at the time, it is unsafe to do so(0018). Use of these facilities is not compulsory, and will depend on your experience, and skills, but they can make your journey safer(0019).

Cycle Tracks, 62(0020).

These are normally located away from the road, but may occasionally be found alongside footpaths, or pavements(0021). Cyclists, and pedestrians may be segregated, or they may share the same space (unsegregated)(0022). When using segregated tracks you MUST keep to the side intended for cyclists, as the pedestrian side remains a pavement, or footpath(0023). Take care when passing pedestrians, especially children, older, or disabled people, and allow them plenty of room(0024). Always be prepared to slow down, and stop if necessary(0025). Take care, near road junctions, as you may have difficulty seeing other road users, who might not notice you(0026).

Law (0027).

 

b0003

Cycle Lanes, 63(0028).

These are marked by a white line (which may be broken) along the carriageway (see Rule 140)(0029). Keep with-in the lane when practicle(0030). When leaving a cycle lane, check before pulling out, that it is safe to do so, and signal your intention, clearly to other road users(0031). Use of cycle lanes is not compulsory, and will depend on your experience, and skills, but they can make your journey safer(0032).

64, (0033).

You MUST NOT cycle on a pavement(0034).

Law (0035).

Bus Lanes, 65(0036).

Most bus lanes may be used by cyclists as indicated on signs(0037). Watch out for people getting on, or off a bus(0038). Be very careful when overtaking a bus, or leaving a bus lane as you will be entering a busier traffic flow(0039). Do not pass between the kerb, and a bus, when it is at a stop(0040).

 

b0004

66, (0041).

You should(0042).

·         keep both hands on the handlebars, except when signalling, or changing gear(0043).

·         keep both feet on the pedals(0044).

·         never ride more than two abreast, and ride in single file on narrow, or busy roads, and when riding round bends(0045).

·         not ride, close behind another vehicle(0046).

·         not carry, anything which will affect your balance, or may get tangled up with your wheels, or chain(0047).

·         be considerate of other road users, particularly blind, and partially sighted pedestrians(0048). Let them know you are there when necessary, for example, by ringing your bell if you have one(0049). It is recommended that a bell be fitted(0050).

 

b0005

67, (0051).

You should(0052).

·         look all around, before moving away from the kerb, turning, or manoeuvring, to make sure it is safe to do so(0053). Give a clear signal, to show other road users, what you intend to do (see Signals to other road users)(0054).

·         look well ahead for obstructions in the road, such as drains, pot-holes, and parked vehicles, so that you do not have to swerve suddenly, to avoid them(0055). Leave plenty of room, when passing parked vehicles, and watch out for doors being opened, or pedestrians stepping into your path(0056).

·         be aware of traffic coming up behind you(0057).

·         take extra care near road humps, narrowings, and other traffic calming features(0058).

·         take care when overtaking (see Rules 162-169)(0059).

 

b0006

68, (0060).

You MUST NOT(0061).

·         carry a passenger, unless your cycle has been built, or adapted to carry one(0062).

·         hold onto, a moving vehicle, or trailer(0063).

·         ride, in a dangerous, careless, or inconsiderate manner(0064).

·         ride, when under the influence of drink, or drugs, including medicine(0065).

Law (0066).

 

b0007

69, (0067).

You MUST obey all traffic signs, and traffic light signals(0068).

Law (0069).

When parking your cycle, 70(0070).

·         find a conspicuous location, where it can be seen by passers-by(0071).

·         use cycle stands, or other cycle parking facilities, wherever possible(0072).

·         do not leave it, where it would cause an obstruction, or hazard to other road users(0073).

·         secure it well, so that it will not fall over, and become an obstruction, or hazard(0074).

 

b0008

71, (0075).

You MUST NOT cross the stop line when the traffic lights are red(0076). Some junctions have an advanced stop line to enable you to wait, and position yourself ahead of other traffic (see Rule 178)(0077).

Law (0078).

 

Road junctions(0079).

On the left, 72(0080).

When approaching a junction on the left, watch out for vehicles turning in front of you, out of, or into the side road(0081). Just before you turn, check for undertaking cyclists, or motor-cyclists(0082). Do not ride on the inside of vehicles signalling, or slowing down to turn left(0083).

 

b0009

73, (0084).

Pay particular attention to long vehicles, which need a lot of room to manoeuvre at corners(0085). Be aware that drivers may not see you(0086). They may have to move over to the right, before turning left(0087). Wait until they have completed the manoeuvre, because the rear wheels come very close to the kerb while turning(0088). Do not be tempted, to ride in the space between them, and the kerb(0089).

On the right, 74(0090).

If you are turning right, check the traffic to ensure it is safe, then signal, and move to the centre of the road(0091). Wait until there is a safe gap, in the oncoming traffic, and give a final look, before completing the turn(0092). It may be safer to wait on the left, until there is a safe gap, or to dismount, and push your cycle across the road(0093).

Dual carriageways, 75(0094).

Remember that traffic on most dual carriageways moves quickly(0095). When crossing, wait for a safe gap, and cross each carriageway in turn(0096). Take extra care when crossing slip roads(0097).

 

b0010

76, (0098).

Full details about the correct procedure at roundabouts, are contained in Rules 184-190(0099). Roundabouts can be hazardous, and should be approached with care(0100).

Roundabouts(0101).

77, (0102).

You may feel safer walking your cycle round on the pavement, or verge(0103). If you decide to ride round keeping to the left-hand lane you should(0104).

·         be aware that drivers may not easily see you(0105).

·         take extra care when cycling across exits(0106). You may need to signal right to show you are not leaving the roundabout(0107).

·         watch out for vehicles crossing your path to leave, or join the roundabout(0108).

78, (0109).

Give plenty of room, to long vehicles on the roundabout, as they need more space to manoeuvre(0110). Do not ride in the space they need, to get round the roundabout(0111). It may be safer to wait until they have cleared the roundabout(0112).

 

b0011

Crossing the road(0113).

79, (0114).

Do not ride across equestrian crossings, as they are for horse riders only(0115). Do not ride across a pelican, puffin, or zebra crossing(0116). Dismount, and wheel your cycle across(0117).

Toucan crossings, 80(0118).

These are light-controlled crossings, which allow cyclists, and pedestrians, to share crossing space, and cross at the same time(0119). They are push-button operated(0120). Pedestrians, and cyclists will see the green signal together(0121). Cyclists are permitted to ride across(0122).

Cycle-only crossings, 81(0123).

Cycle tracks on opposite sides of the road, may be linked, by signalled crossings(0124). You may ride across, but you MUST NOT cross, until the green cycle symbol is showing(0125).

Law (0126).

 

b0012

Level crossings - Tramways, 82(0127).

Take extra care when crossing the tracks (see Rule 306)(0128). You should dismount at level crossings where a cyclist dismount sign is displayed(0129).
