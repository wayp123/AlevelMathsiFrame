Rules for drivers, and motor-cyclists (89-102)---(0001).

Vehicle condition, 89(0002).

You MUST_ ensure your vehicle, and trailer, comply with the full requirements, of the Road Vehicles {Construction, and Use} Regulations, and Road Vehicles Lighting Regulations {see The road user, and the law} (0003).

Fitness to drive(0004).

90, (0005).

Make sure that you are fit to drive(0006). You MUST report to the Driver, and Vehicle Licensing Agency {DVLA} any health condition, likely to affect your driving(0007).

Law (0008).

 

b0002

91, (0009).

Driving when you are tired, greatly increases your risk of collision(0010). To minimise this risk(0011).

·         make sure you are fit to drive(0012). Do not begin a journey if you are tired(0013). Get a good nights sleep before embarking on a long journey(0014).

·         avoid undertaking long journeys, between midnight, and 6 a-m, when natural alertness, is at a minimum(0015).

·         plan your journey to take sufficient breaks(0016). A minimum break of at least, 15 minutes, after every two hours of driving, is recommended(0017).

·         if you feel at all sleepy, stop in a safe place(0018). Do not stop on the hard shoulder of a motorway(0019).

·         the most effective ways to counter sleepiness, are to drink, for example, two cups of caffeinated coffee, and to take a short nap _at least 15 minutes(0020).

 

b0003

Vision, 92(0021).

You MUST_ be able to read a vehicle number plate, in good daylight, from a distance of 20 metres _or 20.5 metres where the old style number plate is used(0022). If you need to wear glasses {or contact lenses} to do this, you MUST wear them at all times, while driving(0023). The police have the power, to require a driver, to undertake an eyesight test(0024).

Law (0025).

93, (0026).

Slow down, and if necessary stop, if you are dazzled by bright sunlight(0027).

94, (0028).

At night, or in poor visibility, do not use tinted glasses, lenses, or visors, if they restrict your vision(0029).

 

b0004

Alcohol, and drugs(0030).

95, (0031).

Do not drink and drive, as it will seriously affect your judgement, and abilities(0032). You MUST NOT drive with a breath alcohol level, higher than 35 micro-grammes per 100 millilitres of breath, or a blood alcohol level, of more than 80 milli-grammes per 100 millilitres of blood(0033). Alcohol will(0034).

·         give a false sense of confidence(0035).

·         reduce co-ordination, and slow down reactions(0036).

·         affect judgement of, speed, distance, and risk(0037).

·         reduce your driving ability, even if youre below the legal limit(0038).

·         take time to leave your body; you may be unfit to drive in the evening, after drinking at lunchtime, or in the morning, after drinking the previous evening(0039).

The best solution is not to drink at all, when planning to drive, because any amount of alcohol, affects your ability to drive safely(0040). If you are going to drink, arrange another means of transport(0041).

Law (0042).

 

b0005

96, (0043).

You MUST NOT drive under the influence of drugs, or medicine(0044). Check the instructions, or ask your doctor, or pharmacist(0045). Using illegal drugs is highly dangerous(0046). Never take them, if you intend to drive; the effects are unpredictable, but can be even more severe than alcohol, and may result in fatal, or serious road crashes(0047).

Law (0048).

97, (0049).

 

b0006

Before setting off(0050). You should ensure that(0051).

·         you have planned your route, and allowed sufficient time(0052).

·         clothing, and footwear, do not prevent you using the controls, in the correct manner(0053).

·         you know where all the controls are, and how to use them before you need them(0054). Not all vehicles are the same; do not wait until it is too late to find out(0055).

·         your mirrors, and seat are adjusted correctly to ensure comfort, full control, and maximum vision(0056).

·         head restraints and seatbelts, are properly adjusted, to reduce the risk of neck, and spine injuries in the event of a collision _see drawing(0057).

·         you have sufficient fuel, oil, and water, before commencing your journey, especially if it includes motorway driving(0058). It can be dangerous to lose power, when driving in traffic(0059).

·         ensure your vehicle is legal, and roadworthy, your tyres are correctly inflated, and all your lights are working(0060).

·         switch off your mobile phone(0061).

 

b0007

Vehicle towing, and loading, 98(0062).

As a driver(0063).

·         you MUST NOT tow , more than your licence permits(0064). If you passed a car test after 1 Jan 1997, you are restricted on the weight of trailer, you can tow(0065).

·         you MUST NOT overload your vehicle, or trailer(0066). You should not tow a weight, greater than that recommended by the manufacturer of your vehicle(0067).

·         you MUST secure your load, and it MUST NOT stick out dangerously(0068). Make sure any heavy, or sharp objects, and any animals are secured safely(0069). If there is a collision, they might hit someone inside the vehicle, and cause serious injury(0070).

·         you should properly distribute the weight in your caravan, or trailer, with heavy items, mainly over the axle{s}, and ensure a downward load on the tow ball(0071). Manufacturers recommended weight, and tow ball load, should not be exceeded(0072). This should avoid the possibility of swerving, or snaking, and going out of control(0073). If this does happen, ease off the accelerator, and reduce speed gently, to regain control(0074).

·         carrying a load, or pulling a trailer, may require you to, adjust the headlights(0075).

 

b0008

In the event of a breakdown, be aware that towing a vehicle on a tow rope, is potentially dangerous(0076). You should consider professional recovery(0077).

Law (0078).
