Motor vehicle documentation, and learner driver requirements(0001).

Documents(0002).

Driving licence(0003).

You MUST have a valid driving licence, for the category of motor vehicle you are driving(0004). You MUST inform the Driver, and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA) if you change your name and, or address(0005).

Law (0006).

Holders of non-European Community licences, who are now resident in the UK, may only drive on that licence for a maximum of 12 months, from the date they become resident in this country(0007). To ensure continuous driving entitlement(0008).

·       a British provisional licence should be obtained, and a driving test(s) passed before the 12-month period elapses, or(0009).

·       in the case of a driver who holds a licence from a country, which has been designated in law, for licence exchange purposes, the driver should exchange the licence for a British one(0010).

 

b0002

M-O-T(0011).

Cars, and motorcycles MUST normally pass an MOT test three years from the date of the first registration, and every year after that(0012). You MUST NOT drive a motor vehicle, without an MOT certificate when it should have one(0013). Exceptionally, you may drive to a pre-arranged test appointment, or to a garage for repairs required for the test(0014). Driving an unroadworthy

 motor vehicle, may invalidate your insurance(0015).

Law (0016).

 

b0003

Insurance(0017).

To use a motor vehicle on the road, you MUST have a valid insurance policy(0018). This MUST at least cover you for injury, or damage to a third party, while using that motor vehicle(0019). Before driving any motor vehicle, make sure that it has this cover for your use, or that your own insurance provides adequate cover(0020). You MUST NOT drive a motor vehicle without insurance(0021). Also, be aware that even if a road traffic incident is not your fault, you may still be held liable by insurance companies(0022).

Law (0023).

Uninsured drivers can now be automatically detected by roadside cameras(0024). Further to the penalties for uninsured driving listed in Penalties, an offenders vehicle can now be seized by the Police, taken away, and crushed(0025).

Law (0026).

The types of cover available are indicated below:(0027).

Third-party insurance - this is often the cheapest form of insurance, and is the minimum cover required by law(0028). It covers anyone you might injure, or whose property you might damage(0029). It does not cover damage to your own motor vehicle, or injury to yourself(0030).

Third-party, Fire, and Theft insurance - similar to third-party, but also covers you against your motor vehicle being stolen, or damaged by fire(0031).

 

b0004

Comprehensive insurance - this is the most expensive but the best insurance(0032). Apart from covering other persons, and property against injury, or damage, it also covers damage to your own motor vehicle, up to the market value of that vehicle, and personal injury to yourself(0033).

Registration certificate(0034).

Registration certificates (also called harmonised registration certificates) are issued for all motor vehicles used on the road, describing them (make, model, etc), and giving details of the registered keeper(0035). You MUST notify the Driver, and Vehicle Licensing Agency, in Swansea, as soon as possible when you buy, or sell a motor vehicle, or if you change your name, or address(0036). For registration certificates issued after 27 March 1997, the buyer, and seller are responsible for completing the registration certificates(0037). The seller is responsible for forwarding them to DVLA(0038). The procedures are explained on the back of the registration certificates(0039).

Law (0040).

 

b0005

Vehicle Excise Duty (VED) (0041).

Vehicle Excise Duty MUST be paid on all motor vehicles used or kept on public roads(0042). See Vehicle tax rate tables(0043).

Law (0044).

 

Statutory Off-Road Notification (SORN) -- (0045).

This is a notification to the DVLA that a motor vehicle is not being used on the road(0046). If you are the vehicle keeper, and want to keep a motor vehicle untaxed, and off the public road you MUST declare SORN - it is an offence not to do so(0047). You then wont have to pay any road tax for that vehicle for a period of 12 months(0048). You need to send a further declaration after that period if the vehicle is still off the public road(0049). The SORN will end if you sell the vehicle, and the new owner will become immediately responsible(0050).

Law (0051).

 

b0006

Production of documents(0052).

You MUST be able to produce your driving licence, a valid insurance certificate, and (if appropriate) a valid MOT certificate, when requested by a police officer(0053). If you cannot do this, you may be asked to take them to a police station within seven days(0054).

Law (0055).

Learner drivers(0056).

Learners driving a car MUST hold a valid provisional licence(0057). They MUST be supervised by someone at least 21 years, old who holds a full EC-EEA licence for that type of car (automatic, or manual), and has held one for at least three years(0058).

Law (0059).

 

b0007

Vehicles(0060).

Any vehicle driven by a learner, MUST display red L plates(0061). In Wales, either red D plates, red L plates, or both, can be used(0062). Plates MUST conform to legal specifications, and MUST be clearly visible to others, from in front of the vehicle, and from behind(0063). Plates should be removed, or covered when not being driven by a learner (except on driving school vehicles)(0064).

Law (0065).

You MUST pass the theory test (if one is required), and then a practical driving test, for the category of vehicle, you wish to drive, before driving unaccompanied(0066).

Law (0067).
