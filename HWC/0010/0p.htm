Rules for users of, powered wheelchairs, and mobility scooters (36-46)---(0001). (9980).

_Called Invalid Carriages, in law_(0002).

36_, (0003).

There is, one class of manual wheelchair {called a Class 1 invalid carriage}, and two classes of,powered wheelchairs, and powered mobility scooters(0004). Manual wheelchairs, and Class 2 vehicles, are those with an upper speed limit of 4 mph {6 km per hour}, and are designed to be used on pavements(0005). Class 3 vehicles, are those with an upper speed limit of 8 mph {12 km per hour}, and are equipped to be used on the road, as well as the pavement(0006).

37, (0007).

When you are on the road you should obey the guidance, and rules for other vehicles; when on the pavement you should follow the guidance, and rules for pedestrians(0008).

 

b0002

On pavements_(0009).

38, (0010).

Pavements are safer than roads, and should be used when available(0011). You should give pedestrians priority, and show consideration for other pavement users, particularly those with a hearing, or visual impairment, who may not be aware that you are there(0012).

39, (0013).

Powered wheelchairs, and scooters MUST NOT travel faster than 4 mph {6 km-h} on pavements, or in pedestrian areas(0014). You may need to reduce your speed, to adjust to other pavement users, who may not be able to move out of your way quickly enough, or where the pavement is too narrow(0015).

Law (0016).

 

b0003

40, (0017).

When moving off the pavement onto the road, you should take special care(0018). Before moving off, always look round, and make sure its safe to join the traffic(0019). Always try to use dropped kerbs, when moving off the pavement, even if this means travelling further to locate one(0020). If you have to climb, or descend a kerb, always approach it at right angles, and dont try to negotiate a kerb higher than the vehicle manufacturers recommendations(0021).

On the road_(0022).

41, (0023).

You should take care when travelling on the road, as you may be travelling more slowly than other traffic {your machine is restricted to 8 mph {12 km-h}, and may be less visible(0024).

 

b0004

42, (0025).

When on the road, Class 3 vehicles should travel in the direction of the traffic(0026). Class 2 users should always use the pavement when it is available(0027). When there is no pavement, you should use caution when on the road(0028). Class 2 users should, where possible, travel in the direction of the traffic(0029). If you are travelling at night, when lights MUST be used, you should travel in the direction of the traffic, to avoid confusing other road users(0030).

Law (0031).

43, (0032).

You MUST follow the same rules about using lights, indicators, and horns, as for other road vehicles, if your vehicle is fitted with them(0033). At night, lights MUST be used(0034). Be aware, that other road users, may not see you, and you should make yourself more visible - even in the daytime, and also at dusk - by, for instance, wearing a reflective jacket, or reflective strips on the back of the vehicle(0035).

Law (0036).

 

b0005

44, (0037).

Take extra care at road junctions(0038). When going straight ahead, check to make sure there are no vehicles about to cross your path from the left, the right, or overtaking you, and turning left(0039).

There are several options for dealing with right turns, especially turning from a major road(0040). If moving into the middle of the road is difficult, or dangerous, you can(0041).

         stop on the left-hand side of the road, and wait for a safe gap in the traffic(0042).

         negotiate the turn as a pedestrian, i e, travel along the pavement, and cross the road between pavements where it is safe to do so(0043). Class 3 users, should switch the vehicle to the lower speed limit, when on pavements(0044).

If the junction is too hazardous, it may be worth considering an alternative route(0045). Similarly, when negotiating major roundabouts {ie, with two, or more lanes} it may be safer for you to use the pavement, or find a route which avoids the roundabout altogether(0046).

 

b0006

45, (0047).

All, normal parking restrictions, should be observed(0048). Your vehicle, should not be left unattended, if it causes an obstruction to other pedestrians - especially those in wheelchairs(0049). Parking concessions, provided under the Blue Badge scheme {see Other information} will apply to those vehicles, displaying a valid badge(0050).

46, (0051).

These vehicles MUST NOT be used on motorways _see Rule 253(0052). They should not be used on unrestricted dual carriageways, where the speed limit exceeds 50 mph {80 km per hour} but if they are used on these dual carriageways, they MUST have a flashing amber beacon(0053). A flashing amber beacon should be used on all other dual carriageways _see Rule 220(0054).

 

Law (0055).
