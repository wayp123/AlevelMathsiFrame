You MUST NOT_(0003).

·         drive dangerously(0004).

·         drive without due care, and attention(0005).

·         drive without reasonable consideration for other road users(0006).

You MUST NOT drive on, or over a pavement, footpath, or bridleway, except to gain lawful access to property, or in the case of an emergency(0009).

b0002

146, (0011).

Adapt your driving to the appropriate type, and condition, of road you are on(0012). In particular(0013).

·         do not treat speed limits as a target(0014). It is often not appropriate, or safe to drive at the maximum speed limit(0015).

·         take the road, and traffic conditions into account(0016). Be prepared for unexpected, or difficult situations, for example, the road being blocked beyond a blind bend(0017). Be prepared to adjust your speed as a precaution(0018).

·         where there are junctions, be prepared for road users emerging(0019).

·         in side roads, and country lanes, look out for unmarked junctions where nobody has priority(0020).

·         be prepared to stop at, traffic control systems, road works, pedestrian crossings, or traffic lights as necessary(0021).

·         try to anticipate what pedestrians, and cyclists might do(0022). If pedestrians, particularly children, are looking the other way, they may step out into the road without seeing you(0023).

 

b0003

Be considerate, 147(0024).

Be careful of, and considerate, towards all types of road users, especially those requiring extra care {see Rule 204}(0025). You should(0026).

·         try to be understanding, if other road users cause problems; they may be inexperienced, or not know the area well(0027).

·         be patient; remember that anyone can make a mistake(0028).

·         not allow yourself to become agitated, or involved if someone is behaving badly on the road(0029). This will only make the situation worse(0030). Pull over, calm down and, when you feel relaxed, continue your journey(0031).

·         slow down, and hold back, if a road user pulls out into your path, at a junction(0032). Allow them to get clear(0033). Do not over-react, by driving too close behind, to intimidate them(0034).

·         not throw anything out of a vehicle, for example, cigarette ends, cans, paper, or carrier bags(0035). This can endanger other road users, particularly motor-cyclists, and cyclists(0036).

 

b0004

148, (0037).

Safe driving, and riding needs concentration(0038).

Avoid distractions when driving, or riding such as(0039).

·         loud music {this may mask other sounds}(0040).

·         trying to read maps(0041).

·         inserting a cassette, or CD, or tuning a radio(0042).

·         arguing with your passengers, or other road users(0043).

·         eating, and drinking(0044).

·         smoking(0045).

You MUST NOT smoke in public transport vehicles, or in vehicles used for work purposes, in certain prescribed circumstances(0046). Separate regulations apply to England, Wales, and Scotland(0047). In England and Wales, the driver MUST NOT smoke, or allow anyone, to smoke, in an enclosed private vehicle, carrying someone under 18, including motor caravans(0073). In Scotland, it is an offence for anyone, aged 18 or over, to smoke in a private motor vehicle {unless it is parked, and being used as living accommodation} when there is someone, under 18 in the vehicle, and the vehicle, is in a public place(0074).

Law (0048).

 

b0005

Mobile phones, and in-vehicle technology(0049).

149, (0050).

You MUST exercise proper control of your vehicle at all times(0051). You MUST NOT use a hand-held mobile phone, or similar device, when driving, or waiting in traffic, or when supervising a learner driver, except to call 999, or 112 in a genuine emergency, when it is unsafe, or impractical to stop(0052). You should not use a hand-held microphone when driving(0053). Using hands-free equipment, is also likely to distract your attention from the road(0054). It is far safer, not to use any telephone, while you are driving, or riding - find a safe place to stop first, or use the voicemail facility, and listen to messages later(0055).

Law (0056).

150, (0057).

There is a danger of driver distraction being caused by in-vehicle systems, such as satellite navigation systems, congestion warning systems, PCs, multi-media, etc(0058). You MUST exercise_ proper control of your vehicle, at all times_(0059). Do not rely on driver assistance systems, such as cruise control, or lane departure warnings(0060). They are available to assist, but you should not reduce your concentration levels(0061). Do not be distracted by maps, or screen-based information {such as navigation, or vehicle management systems} while driving, or riding(0062). If necessary, find a safe place to stop(0063).

Law (0064).

 

b0006

In slow-moving traffic, 151(0065).

You should_(0066).

·         reduce the distance between you, and the vehicle ahead, to maintain traffic flow(0067).

·         never get so close to the vehicle in front, that you cannot stop safely(0068).

·         leave enough space to be able to manoeuvre, if the vehicle in front breaks down, or an emergency vehicle needs to get past(0069).

·         not change lanes to the left, to overtake(0070).

·         allow access into, and from side roads, as blocking these will add to congestion {see drawing}(0071).

·         be aware of cyclists, and motor-cyclists, who may be passing on either side(0072).
