Driving in adverse weather conditions (226-237)---(0001).

226, (0002).

You MUST use headlights, when visibility is seriously reduced, generally when you cannot see for more than 100 metres (328 feet)(0003). You may also use front, or rear fog lights, but you MUST switch them off when visibility improves (see Rule 236)(0004).

Law (0005).

Wet weather, 227(0006).

In wet weather, stopping distances will be at least double those required for stopping on dry roads (see Rule 126)(0007). This is because your tyres have less grip on the road(0008).

 

b0002

In wet weather(0009).

·         you should keep well back from the vehicle in front(0010). This will increase your ability to see, and plan ahead(0011).

·         if the steering becomes unresponsive, it probably means that water is preventing the tyres from gripping the road(0012). Ease off the accelerator, and slow down gradually(0013).

·         the rain, and spray from vehicles may make it difficult to see, and be seen (0014).

·         be aware of the dangers of spilt diesel that will make the surface very slippery (see Vehicle maintenance, safety, and security)(0015).

·         take extra care around pedestrians, cyclists, motor-cyclists, and horse riders(0016).

 

b0003

Icy, and snowy weather(0017).

228, (0018).

In winter, check the local weather forecast, for warnings of icy, or snowy weather(0019). DO NOT drive in these conditions unless your journey is essential(0020). If it is, take great care, and allow more time for your journey(0021). Take an emergency kit of de-icer, and ice scraper, torch, warm clothing, and boots, first aid kit, jump leads, and a shovel, together with a warm drink, and emergency food, in case you get stuck, or your vehicle breaks down(0022).

229, (0023).

 

b0004

Before you set off(0024).

·         you MUST be able to see, so clear all snow, and ice from all your windows (see drawing)(0025).

·         you MUST ensure that lights are clean, and number plates are clearly visible, and legible(0026).

·         make sure the mirrors are clear, and the windows are demisted thoroughly(0027).

·         remove all snow, that might fall off, into the path of other road users(0028).

·         check your planned route is clear of delays, and that no further snowfalls, or severe weather are predicted(0029).

Law (0030).

230, (0031).

 

b0005

When driving in icy, or snowy weather(0032).

·         drive with care, even if the roads have been treated(0033).

·         keep well back from the road user in front, as stopping distances can be ten times greater, than on dry roads(0034).

·         take care when overtaking vehicles spreading salt, or other de-icer, particularly if you are riding a motorcycle, or cycle(0035).

·         watch out for snowploughs, which may throw out snow on either side(0036). Do not overtake, them unless the lane you intend to use, has been cleared(0037).

·         be prepared for the road conditions to change, over relatively short distances(0038).

·         listen to travel bulletins, and take note of variable message signs, that may provide information, about weather, road, and traffic conditions ahead(0039).

 

b0006

231, (0040).

Drive extremely carefully when the roads are icy(0041). Avoid sudden actions, as these could cause loss of control(0042).

You should(0043).

·         drive at a slow speed, in as high a gear as possible; accelerate, and brake very gently(0044).

·         drive particularly slowly on bends, where loss of control is more likely(0045). Brake progressively on the straight, before you reach a bend(0046). Having slowed down, steer smoothly round the bend, avoiding sudden actions(0047).

·         check your grip on the road surface, when there is snow, or ice, by choosing a safe place to brake gently(0048). If the steering feels unresponsive, this may indicate ice, and your vehicle losing its grip, on the road(0049). When travelling on ice, tyres make virtually no noise(0050).

 

b0007

Windy weather(0051).

232, (0052).

High-sided vehicles are most affected by windy weather, but strong gusts can also blow a car, cyclist, motorcyclist, or horse rider, off course(0053). This can happen on open stretches of road, exposed to strong crosswinds, or when passing bridges, or gaps in hedges(0054).

233, (0055).

In very windy weather, your vehicle may be affected by turbulence, created by large vehicles(0056). Motor-cyclists are particularly affected, so keep well back from them, when they are overtaking a high-sided vehicle(0057).

Fog, (0058).

234, (0059).

Before entering fog, check your mirrors, then slow down(0060). If the word Fog is shown on a roadside signal, but the road is clear, be prepared for a bank of fog, or drifting patchy fog ahead(0061). Even if it seems to be clearing, you can suddenly find yourself in thick fog(0062).

235, (0063).

 

b0008

When driving in fog you should(0064).

·         use your lights as required (see Rule 226)(0065).

·         keep a safe distance behind the vehicle in front(0066). Rear lights can give a false sense of security(0067).

·         be able to pull up, well within the distance, you can see clearly(0068). This is particularly important on motorways, and dual carriageways, as vehicles are travelling faster(0069).

·         use your windscreen wipers, and demisters(0070).

·         beware of other drivers not using headlights(0071).

·         not accelerate, to get away from a vehicle, which is too close behind you(0072).

·         check your mirrors, before you slow down(0073). Then use your brakes, so that your brake lights warn drivers behind you, that you are slowing down(0074).

·         stop in the correct position at a junction with limited visibility, and listen for traffic(0075). When you are sure it is safe to emerge, do so positively, and do not hesitate in a position that puts you directly in the path of approaching vehicles(0076).

 

b0009

236, (0077).

You MUST NOT use front, or rear fog lights unless visibility is seriously reduced (see Rule 226) as they dazzle other road users, and can obscure your brake lights(0078). You MUST switch them off when visibility improves(0079).

Law (0080).

Hot weather, 237(0081).

Keep your vehicle well ventilated to avoid drowsiness(0082). Be aware that the road surface may become soft, or if it rains after a dry spell, it may become slippery(0083). These conditions could affect your steering, and braking(0084). If you are dazzled by bright sunlight, slow down, and if necessary, stop(0085).
