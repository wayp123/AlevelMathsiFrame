Roundabouts (184-190)---(0001).

184, (0002).

On approaching a roundabout, take notice, and act on all the information available to you, including traffic signs, traffic lights, and lane markings, which direct you into the correct lane(0003).

You should(0004).

·         use Mirrors - Signal - Manoeuvre at all stages(0005).

·         decide as early as possible, which exit you need to take(0006).

·         give an appropriate signal (see Rule 186)(0007). Time your signals, so as not to confuse, other road users(0008).

·         get into the correct lane(0009).

·         adjust your speed, and position, to fit in with traffic conditions(0010).

·         be aware of the speed, and position, of all the road users around you(0011).

 

b0002

185, (0012).

When reaching the roundabout, you should(0013).

·         give priority to traffic approaching from your right, unless directed otherwise by signs, road markings, or traffic lights(0014).

·         check whether road markings, allow you to enter the roundabout, without giving way(0015). If so, proceed, but still look to the right, before joining(0016).

·         watch out for all other road users already on the roundabout; be aware they may not be signalling correctly, or at all(0017).

·         look forward, before moving off, to make sure traffic in front has moved off(0018).

 

186, (0019).

 

b0003

Signals, and position(0020).

When taking the first exit to the left, unless signs, or markings indicate otherwise(0021).

·         signal left, and approach in the left-hand lane (0022).

·         keep to the left on the roundabout, and continue signalling left to leave(0023).

When taking an exit to the right, or going full circle, unless signs, or markings indicate otherwise(0024).

·         signal right, and approach in the right-hand lane(0025).

·         keep to the right on the roundabout, until you need to change lanes, to exit the roundabout(0026).

·         signal left, after you have passed the exit, before the one, you want(0027).

When taking any intermediate exit, unless signs, or markings indicate otherwise(0028).

·         select the appropriate lane on approach to the roundabout(0029).

·         you should not normally need to signal on approach(0030).

·         stay in this lane, until you need to alter course, to exit the roundabout(0031).

·         signal left, after you have passed the exit, before the one, you want(0032).

When there are more than three lanes, at the entrance to a roundabout, use the most appropriate lane on approach, and through it(0033).

 

b0004

187, (0034).

In all cases watch out for, and give plenty of room to(0035).

·         pedestrians, who may be crossing the approach, and exit roads(0036).

·         traffic, crossing in front of you, on the roundabout, especially vehicles intending to leave by the next exit(0037).

·         traffic, which may be straddling lanes, or positioned incorrectly(0038).

·         motor-cyclists(0039).

·         cyclists, and horse riders, who may stay in the left-hand lane, and signal right if they intend to continue round the roundabout(0040). Allow them to do so(0041).

·         long vehicles, (including those towing trailers)(0042). These might have to take a different course, or straddle lanes, either approaching, or on the roundabout, because of their length(0043). Watch out for their signals(0044).
