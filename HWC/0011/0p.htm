Rules about animals (47-58)---(0001).

Horse-drawn vehicles, 47(0002).

Horse-drawn vehicles, used on the highway, should be operated, and maintained, in accordance with standards, set out in the Department for Transports Code of Practice, for Horse-Drawn Vehicles(0003). This Code lays down the requirements for a road driving assessment, and includes a comprehensive list of safety checks to ensure that a carriage, and its fittings are safe, and in good working order(0004). The standards set out in the Road Driving Assessment, may be required to be met by a Local Authority, if an operator wishes to obtain a local authority licence, to operate a passenger-carrying service _see Other information(0005).

Safety equipment, and clothing, 48(0006).

All horse-drawn vehicles should have, two, red, rear, reflectors(0007). It is safer not to drive at night, but if you do, a light showing white to the front, and red to the rear MUST be fitted(0008).

Horse riders(0010).

Safety equipment, 49(0011).

Children under the age of 14, MUST wear a helmet, which complies with the Regulations(0012). It MUST be fastened securely(0013). Other riders should also follow these requirements(0014). These requirements do not apply to a child, who is a follower of the Sikh religion, while wearing a turban(0015).

Law (0016).

Other clothing, 50(0017).

You should wear(0018).

·         boots, or shoes with hard soles, and heels(0019).

·         light-coloured, or fluorescent clothing in daylight(0020).

·         reflective clothing, if you have to ride at night, or in poor visibility(0021).

 

b0003

At night, 51(0022).

It is safer not to ride on the road at night, or in poor visibility, but if you do, make sure you wear reflective clothing, and your horse has reflective bands above the fetlock joints _see drawing(0023). A light which shows white to the front, and red to the rear should be fitted, with a band, to the riders right arm, and, or, leg, or riding boot(0024). If you are leading a horse at night, carry a light in your right hand, showing white to the front, and red to the rear, and wear reflective clothing on both you, and your horse(0025). It is strongly recommended that a fluorescent, or reflective, tail guard, is also worn by your horse(0026).

Riding, 52(0027).

Before you take a horse on to a road, you should(0028).

·         ensure all tack fits well, and is in good condition(0029).

·         make sure you can control the horse(0030).

Always ride with other, less nervous horses if you think that your horse will be nervous of traffic(0031). Never ride a horse without both a saddle, and bridle(0032).

53, (0033).

Before riding off, or turning, look behind you to make sure it is safe, then give a clear arm signal(0034).

 

b0004

When riding on the road you should(0035).

·         keep to the left(0036).

·         keep both hands on the reins, unless you are signalling(0037).

·         keep both feet in the stirrups(0038).

·         not carry another person(0039).

·         not carry anything, which might affect your balance, or get tangled up with the reins(0040).

·         keep a horse you are leading, to your left(0041).

·         move in the direction of the traffic flow, in a one-way street(0042).

·         never ride more than two abreast, and ride in single file on narrow, or busy roads, and when riding round bends(0043).

 

b0005

54, (0044).

You MUST NOT take a horse onto a footpath, or pavement, and you should not take a horse onto a cycle track(0045). Use a bridleway where possible(0046). Equestrian crossings may be provided for horse riders to cross the road, and you should use these where available _see Rule 27(0047). You should dismount at level crossings, where a horse rider dismount sign is displayed(0048).

Law (0049).

Avoid roundabouts wherever possible, 55(0050).

 If you use them you should(0051).

·         keep to the left, and watch out for vehicles crossing your path, to leave, or join the roundabout(0052).

·         signal right, when riding across exits, to show you are not leaving(0053).

·         signal left, just before you leave the roundabout(0054).

 

b0006

Other animals_(0055).

Dogs, 56(0056).

Do not let a dog out on the road, on its own(0057). Keep it, on a short lead, when walking on the pavement, road, or path shared with cyclists, or horse riders(0058).

57, (0059).

When in a vehicle, make sure dogs, or other animals, are suitably restrained, so they cannot distract you, while you are driving, or injure you, or themselves, if you stop quickly(0060). A seat belt harness, pet carrier, dog cage, or dog guard, are ways of restraining animals in cars(0061).

Animals being herded, 58(0062).

These should be kept under control at all times(0063). You should, if possible, send another person along the road in front, to warn other road users, especially at a bend, or the brow of a hill(0064). It is safer not to move animals after dark, but if you do, then wear reflective clothing, and ensure that lights are carried {white at the front, and red at the rear}, of the herd(0065).
