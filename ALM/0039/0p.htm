Derivative_(0001). (9980).

 

The derivative function , is the rate of change function, of a function(0002).

 

The derivative of a function, is the new function,

f dash x, = the limit of, f x, plus h, minus f x over, h, as h approaches 0(0003).

 

To see how to get this, see later page, Derivative Explained.(0004).

 

 

b0002

 

A function is differentiable at the point A, only if the limit exists at the point A(0005).

 

The notation for the derivative is,

 

d y, d x, or f dash x(0006).

 

 

b0003

 

Some Differentiation Rules on polynomials(0007).

 

1, Derivative of a constant function:

d, dx, c = 0(0008).

 

2, The Power, or indices Rule:

d, dx, x n, = n, x, n minus 1(0009).

 

3, The Constant Multiple Rule, if c is a constant and, fx, is a differentiable function:

d, dx, c fx, = c, d, dx, fx(0010).

 

  

b0004

 

4, The Sum, and Difference Rule:

d, dx, fx plus gx, = d dx, fx, plus d dx, g x(0011).

 

 

d, dx, fx minus gx, = D dx, fx, minus d, dx g x(0012).

 

The above 4 rules, can all be applied, at the same time, to one function(0013). The function must be simplified to the standard polynomial form(0014).

g x = x cubed, minus 3, h x = x squared, 5, I x = x, minus 1, j x = 0(0020).

Gradient of a Line_(0023).

 

The derivative is also used to find the gradient of a line, at a point A(0024). Then the point A, is substituted into the derivative(0025). The result will give us the gradient, at the point A(0026).

 

Note: The first derivative of a distance function, is the velocity function, and the second derivative is acceleration(0027).

This is so, because velocity is distance over time, or the slope of a distance function, and acceleration is the slope of the velocity function, or the second derivative of the distance function(0028).

 

b0008

 

Example: The distance that a car has travelled, is given by a function(0029).

s = 1.25 t squared minus 5 T(0030). S, is the distance travelled in metres, and t, is the time elapsed, in seconds (0031). What is the velocity of the car when 12 seconds have elapsed?(0032).

 

1, Using the power, the constant multiple rule, and the difference derivative rules, and givens= f, t, we get(0033).

v = 2.5t minus 5, since v,= f, d T, the velocity function(0034).

 

2, Now we substitute the time, 12 into the function to get:

v = 2.5 times 12 minus 5, = 25 metres per second, at 12 seconds(0035).

Tangent Lines(0036).

 

Since we can find the gradient of a function at any point, we can also find the equation of the tangent line to the function, at any point(0037).

 

What is the equation of the tangent line for the curve,

y = x squared plus 3x minus 16, at the point, x = minus 4?(0038).

 

First we need to find the derivative of the function, to get the gradient function, which is,

d y, d x, = 2x plus 3(0039).

 

Then the gradient, at the point, x = -4 is,

2 times neg 4 plus 3 = minus 5(0040).

 

b0010

 

Using the original function we find the corresponding y value, to the x value, at the point of tangency, so y, at x = minus 4, is(0041).

y= minus 4 squared plus 3, minus 4, minus 16 = minus 12(0042).

 

Then we use the point-gradient equation, to find the tangent line equation, using the gradient, and the complete point we found,

y plus 12 = minus 5x plus 4(0043).

 

so the equation of the tangent line is

y = minus 5x minus 32(0044).

 

b0011

 

Normal Lines(0045).

 

A Normal line, is the line perpendicular to the Tangent Line(0046). Therefore when we determine the equation of the normal line, the procedure is very similar to determining the equation of the tangent line(0047).

 

b0012

 

Example: What is the equation of the normal line for the curve

y = x squared plus 3x minus 16, at the point, minus 4, minus 12?(0048).

 

First we need to find the derivative of the function,

d y, d x, = 2x plus 3(0049).

 

Then we need to find the slope at the given x value,

2 times neg 4 plus 3 = minus 5(0050).

 

Then we use the perpendicular line equation to find the gradient of the normal line(0051).

 

m1 times m2, = minus 1, so minus 5, times m2, = minus 1(0052).

 

b0013

 

m2= minus 1 over, -5 so the gradient is 1 over, 5(0053).

 

Then we find the y value corresponding to the x value,

y= minus 4 squared plus 3 times minus 4 minus 16= minus 12(0054). We did not have to do this step because the x y point was already given above(0055).

 

Then we use the point-gradient equation to find the equation(0056).

y plus 12= point 2 times x plus 4(0057).

 

so the equation of the normal line is

y = point 2x minus 11.2 (0058).

Application of Derivatives to Graphs(0065).

 

Derivatives also help us to graph functions, by locating minimum, maximum, and inflection points(0066). They can also determine the interval on which a function is convex, or concave(0067). For a quadratic, it is convex, or concave, over, the whole interval(0068).

 

Convex_- The graph is below the tangent lines, or upside-down, bucket shape(0069).

b0017

 

Inflection Point_ - The point when a function changes from convex to concave, or vice versa(0073). The slope of a convex function, is decreasing, as x is increasing, and the slope of a concave function, is increasing, as x is increasing(0074). So, at an inflection point, the change of slope = 0, or  f2x = 0(0075). Example, x cubed, at x = 0(0076).

 

Stationary Point - A point where

 f dash c = 0, or where the slope =0, this also occurs at the maximum, or minimum points, on the graph(0079).

 

Maximum Point_ - The highest point of a function, found on a convex interval(0077).

 

Minimum Point_ - The lowest point of a function, found on a concave interval(0078).
