Volumes of Revolution(0001).

Integrals are used to find the volume of a shape, that is created by rotating a line, or set of lines, around the x, or y axiss(0002). We can only, revolve around an axis, that is independent(0003). This is also the way to prove, the formulae, for the volumes, of such shapes as, cones and spheres(0004). The method that you will learn, in this module, is known as the disk method(0005).

 

b0002

 

The formulae are(0006).

 

y=fx, Around the x axis,

v x, = pi, from A, to b, y squared, d x(0007).

 

x=f y, Around the y axis,

v y, = pi, from c, to d, x squared, d y(0008).

 

 

b0003

 

The procedure is(0009).

1, Square the function, and integrate, as per the, formula(0010).

2, Input the highest value(0011).

3, Input the lower value(0012).

4, Subtract the lower result, from the higher, so the answer is positive(0013).

5, If the curve, is bounded by another curve, do steps 1 to 5, and then subtract the lower curve, from the higher curve(0014). The bound region is, A, at the highest point, at which the two curves meet, and b, is the lowest point, at which the two curves meet again(0015).

 

b0004

 

Example One: Area between a Curve, and an Axis(0016).

 

Find the volume of the solid obtained by rotating the horizontal line,

y=, 2 over, 2x plus 9(0017).

 

around the x-axis, and bounded between, by the vertical line,  x = 6, and the y, axis(0018).

 

b0005

 

1, First we square the function and integrate,

pi, 0 to 6, 2 over, 2x plus 9, squared, dx(0019).

 

Simplify with multiply, and get square on, only 1 side of fraction,

pi, 0 to 6, 4, over, 2x plus 9, squared, dx(0020).

 

b0006 

 

After using linear substitution, we get,

pi, neg 2 over, 2x plus 9, from 0 to 6(0021).

 

2, Then we input the highest value 6,

neg 2 over, 2 times 6, plus 9 = neg 2 over 21(0022).

 

3, Then we input the lowest value 0,

neg 2 over, 2 times 0, plus 9 = neg 2 over 9(0023).

 

b0007

 

4, Finally we subtract the result from the higher value from the result of the lower value(0024). The answer has to be positive(0025).

 

pi, neg 2 over 21, minus, neg 2 over 9, = 8 over 63, pi(0026).

5, The area of the solid is, 8 over 63, pi_(0027).

 

b0008

 

Example Two: Area between Two Curves(0028).

 

Find the volume of the object bounded by the curves,

y= x squared(0029).

 

and_,

y= the square root of x(0030).

 

both revolved horizontaly around the x-axis(0031).

 

b0009

 

1, First we find where they are equal, or meet, to find the vertical range(0032).

 

 x squared, = the square root of x, when x = 1, or 0(0033).

 

2, Then we integrate the first function,

pi, 0 to 1, x4, dx(0034).

 

pi, x5 over 5, 0 to 1(0035).

 

b0010

 

3, Now we input the higher number,

1 5, over 5, = 1 over 5(0036).

 

4, Then we input the lower number,

0 5, over 5, = 0 over 5(0037).

 

5, We subtract and we get,

 

1 pi, over 5(0038).

 

b0011

 

6, Now we need to do the same for the second curve(0039).

 

7, We integrate the second function,

pi, 0 to 1, x dx(0040).

 

pi, x squared, over 2, 0 to 1(0041).

 

8, Now we input the higher number,

1 2, over 2, = 1 over 2(0042).

 

b0012

 

9, Then we input the lower number,

0 2, over 2, = 0 over 2(0043).

10, We subtract and we get,  1 pi, over 2_(0044).

 

b0013

 

11, Finally we subtract the lower, {smaller area} curve, from the area of the upper curve,

1 pi, over 2, minus 1 pi, over 5, = 3 pi, over 10(0045).

 

12, The area of the solid, bounded by the curves,

x squared, and the square root of x(0046).

 

and rotated around the x-axis is, 3 pi, over 10_(0047).
