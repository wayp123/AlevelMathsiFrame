The Reciprocal, Trigonometric Function(0001).

A reciprocal of a function, is a 1 over function, not an inverse function, so when you multiply the reciprocal, by the function, your resultant is one(0002). Since you can never have a zero, in the denominator, the reciprocal function, will approach asymptotes, at the point, when the function is zero(0003). These are the blue lines in the graph(0004). Another relationship to remember is, the smaller the value of the original function, the larger the reciprocal, function value, will be(0005). This applies to any function, not just the trig functions(0006).
