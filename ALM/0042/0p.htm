Differentiation, and the derivative(0001). (9979).

At each point, of a function curve, the derivative is, the slope of a line, that is at a tangent to the curve(0002). The blue line, is always a tangent, to the red curve; its slope is the derivative(0003).

Differentiation is a method to compute the rate at which a quantity, y, changes with respect to the change, in another quantity, x, upon which it is dependent(0004). This rate of change is called the derivative of y with respect to x(0005). In more precise language, the dependency of y on x, means that y, is a function of x(0006). If x, and y are real numbers, and if the graph of y is plotted against x, the derivative measures the slope of this graph at each point(0007). This functional relationship is often denoted, y = f_x_, where f, denotes the function(0008).

 

b0002

The simplest case is when y is a linear function of x, meaning that the graph of y against x, is a straight line(0009). In this case,  y = f_x_ = m x plus c(0010).

For any real numbers, m, and c, the slope m, is given by,

m,= change in y over, change in x, = delta y over, delta x(0011).

 

b0003

The delta symbol is an abbreviation for change in(0012).

This formula is true because,

 

y plus delta y, = f, x plus delta x, = m, x plus delta x, plus c, = m x plus c, plus m delta x, = y plus m, delta x(0013).

 

It follows that,

 

delta y = m delta x(0014).

 

b0004

This means that the change of y, also depends on m, as x changes, and the rate of change, or the slope, of a straight line, is constant, and is equal to m(0015). If the function f, is not linear _that is its graph is not a straight line_, then the change in y, divided by the change in x, varies(0016). Differentiation is a method to find an exact value, for this rate of change, at any given value of x(0017).

The idea, illustrated by Figures 1-3, is to compute the rate of change, as the limiting value of the ratio of the differences, delta y, over, delta x, as delta x, becomes infinitely small(0018).

b0005

 

In Leibnizs notation, such an infinitesimal change in x, is denoted by dx, and the derivative of y, with respect to x, is written,

 

d y , dx(0019).

 

This suggests the ratio of two infinitesimal quantities(0020).

The above expression is pronounced in various ways such as, d y, by d x, or d y, over, d x(0021). The oral form, d y, d x is often used conversationally, although it may lead to confusion(0022).

 

b0006

 

Definition via difference quotients(0023).

 

Let y=f_x_ be a function of x(0024). The derivative of y with respect to x at A, is, geometrically, the slope of the tangent line to the graph of f, at A(0025). The slope of the tangent line, is very close to the slope of the line connecting the point,  A, f A, and a nearby point on the graph, for example A, plus h, f, A plus h(0026). These lines are called secant lines(0027). A value of h, close to zero, will give a good approximation to the slope of the tangent line, and smaller values _in absolute value_ of h, will, in general, give better approximations(0028). The slope m, of the secant line, is also the difference between the y values of these points, divided by the difference between the x values(0029).

 

b0007

 

That is,

m= delta y over, delta x, = delta f x over, delta x, = f A,plus h, minus f A, over, h(0030).

 

This expression is Newtons difference, or delta quotient(0031). The derivative is the value of the difference quotient, as the secant lines, get closer, and closer to the tangent line(0032).

 

b0009

 

Formally, the derivative of the function f, at A, is,

f 1 A, = the limit of f A,plus h, minus f A, over, h, as h approaches 0(0033).

 

If the limit exists, then f is differentiable at A(0034). Here, f, 1 A, is one of several common notations for the derivative(0035).

 

b0010

 

Substituting 0 for h, in the difference quotient, causes division by zero, so the slope of the tangent line cannot be found directly(0036). Instead, define  Q h, to be the difference quotient, as a function of h:(0037).

Q h = f A,plus h, minus f A, over, h(0038).

 

Q h is the slope of the secant line, between,

A, f A, and, A plus h, f, A plus h(0039).

 

b0011

 

If f, is a continuous function, meaning that its graph, is an unbroken curve, with no gaps, then Q is a continuous function, away from the point h = 0_(0040).

 

If the limit Q h exists as h approaches 0, means there is a way of choosing a value for Q 0_(0041). This means, if the graph of Q is a continuous function, then the function f, is differentiable at the point A, and its derivative at A, equals Q 0(0042).

 

In practice, the existence, of a continuous extension of the difference quotient, Q h, to h = 0, is shown by modifying the numerator to cancel h, in the denominator(0043). This process can be long, and tedious for complicated functions, and many short cuts are commonly used to simplify the process(0044).
