Inequalities_(0001).

 

An inequality, is an expression, which compares, the relative sizes of, points, lines, or curves(0002). Unlike an equation, where both sides of the equals sign are always equal, inequalities, can have one side greater than, or equal to the other side(0003).

 

The four signs of inequalities(0004).

 

b0002

 

There are four main basic signs:(0005).

 

 less than_(0006).

 greater than_(0007).

 less than, or equal to_(0008).

 greater than, or equal to(0009).

 

For example(0010).

 x is less than 4(0011).

 x is greater than 4(0012).

 x is 4, or any number less than, or smaller than 4(0013).

 x is 4, or any number greater than, or higher than 4(0014).

 

b0003

 

Note that,

x is greater than y, and y is less than x, are both essentially the same statement(0015).

 

If you become confused with which sign means, less than, and greater than, it is useful to remember that the inequality signs always point to the smaller number(0016). One basic rule to remember is that if you divide, or multiply, by a negative quantity, the inequality sign reverses(0017).

 

For example_,

minus x greater than, or = to 7, will become x is less than, or equal to minus 7(0018). This makes sense because -4, is greater than -5, but 4, is less than 5(0019).

Combining inequalities(0020).

 

There are some cases where two, inequalities can be combined into one(0021). For example, the height of the door was said to be 

 greater, or equal to 1.95, and 

 less than 2.05(0022).

The usual way of writing these is,

 x greater than, or equal to 1.95, and x is less than 2.05_(0023). Notice that the inequality signs are in the same direction(0024).

 x less than, or equal to 2.05, and x is greater than 1.95 is perfectly acceptable, but it is incorrect to combine opposite facing inequalities, they must be left as two seperate inequalities(0025).

Solving Linear Inequalities, or equations(0026).

 

In order to solve a linear inequality simply isolate x(0027). For example:

minus 2x minus 5 is greater than, or = to 0(0028).

becomes minus 2x is greater than, or = to 5(0029).

x is less than, or = to minus 5 over, 2, Note the sign change(0030).

Solving Quadratic Inequalities(0031).

 

In order to solve quadratic inequalities we need to:(0032).

 

1, Set the inequality to zero(0033).

 

2, Factor the equation, and find the zeros, or single zero(0034).

 

3, Make an x axis number line, with the zeros of the equation as the only points, Make intervals between these points(0035).

 

4, Find the sign of the factors, Then find the sign of the equation on the interval(0036).

 

5, The intervals, or interval of x, that satisfy the inequality, is your answer set(0037).

 

b0007

 

For example: Find the intervals, that satisfy the relationship(0038).

x squared minus 4 x is greater than, or = to 5(0039).

 

Step 1_:,

x squared minus 4 x minus 5 is greater than, or equal to 0(0040).

Step 2:,

the factors are x plus 1, and x minus 5 that are greater than, or = to 0(0041).

So the zeros will be -1, and 5, and values of the factors to the left of their zeros will be negative, and to the right, positive(0042).

 

Step 3, and 4:,

make a number line, and find the sign of the factors, then of the equation on the interval(0043).

 

Step 5: The intervals that satisfy the relationship greater than zero are,

x is less than, or equal to minus 1, and x is greater, or equal to 5(0044). This is the solution set since they are both positive, or greater than 0 as required by the inequality as seen in step 1, or 2(0045).

Solving Inequalities involving Fractions(0046).

 

Solving an equation involving a fraction is very similar to solving a quadratic inequality(0047). When you solve an inequality involving fractions, you cannot cross multiply _because you could be multiplying by a negative number which would reverse the sign of the inequality(0048). This will lead to an erroneous answer(0049). Also remember that you can never have a zero in the denominator(0050).

 

b0010

 

For example: Find the intervals that satisfy the relationship,

1 over, x plus 1 is greater than, or = to 6 minus x over, x plus 1(0051).

 

Step 1: set to 0, and simplified, it becomes,

1 over, x plus 1 minus 6 minus x over, x plus 1 is greater than, or = to 0, becomes(0052).

x minus 5 over, x plus 1 is greater than, or = to 0(0053).

 

b0011

 

Step 2: It is already factored, so the zeros are -1, and 5(0054).

 

Step 3, and 4:,

 

make a number line, and find the sign of the factors, then of the equation on the interval(0055).

 

Step 5: The intervals that satisfy the relationship are

 x is less than minus 1, and x is greater, or equal to 5(0056). Note that  x is less than minus 1, because x cannot equal to -1, or else there will be a zero in the denominator(0057).

In interval notation the solution set is given in brackets(0061). _also order the original intervals in numerical order(0062). There are two types of brackets that are used:(0063).
