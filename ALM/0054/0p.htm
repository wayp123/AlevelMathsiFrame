The Law of Cosines_(0001).

 

Pythagoras theory, only applies to right triangles, the law of cosines, will apply to any triangle(0002). When you have a right triangle, it reduces, to the same formula, as given by Pythagoras theorem(0003).

 

For any triangle,  A, B, C, with angle measurement, alpha, beta, gamma, and sides, of length, A, b, c(0004). Use mostly, when given 1 angle, and 2 sides, to find the other side(0005).

 

 

b0002

 

 

A, squared = b squared, plus c squared, minus 2 b c, cos, alpha(0006).

b, squared = A, squared, plus c squared, minus 2 A, c, cos, beta(0007).

c, squared = A, squared, plus b squared, minus 2 A, b, cos, gamma(0008).

 

b0003

 

Example_(0009).

What is the value of c, when A, = 4 cm,  b, = 8 cm, and gamma, is equal to 64, degrees(0010).

 

c, squared = 4 squared, plus 8 squared, minus 2, times 4, times 8, cos 64 degrees(0011).

c, squared = 53(0012).

c, = 7.28 cm(0013).

The Law of Sines(0014).

For any triangle,  A, B, C, with angle measurement, alpha, beta, gamma, and sides, of length, A, b, c(0015). Use mostly, when given 2, angles, and a side, to find the other side(0016).

 

A, over sine alpha, = b, over sine beta, = c, over sine gamma(0017).

 

b0005

 

Example(0018).

If Angle, alpha, is 45 degrees, Angle beta is, 24 degrees, and Side, b, is 3 cm, what is the length, of side A_(0019).

 

A, over sine 45 degrees, = 3 over sine 24 degrees(0020).

 

A, times sine 24 degrees, = 3 times sine 45 degrees(0021).

 

A, = 3 times, sine 45 degrees over sine 24 degrees, = 5.22 cm(0022).

Area, of a Triangle_(0023).

For, any triangle, the area, is one-half, the product, of two sides, with the sine of the included angle(0024). If, the included angle, is a right angle, then this reduces, to the formula, for the area, of a right triangle, since  sine 90 degrees = 1(0025).

 

b0007

 

Area, = 1 over 2, b c, sine alpha(0026).

 

Area, = 1 over 2, A, c, sine beta(0027).

 

Area, = 1 over 2, A, b, sine gamma(0028).

 

b0008

 

Example(0029).

What is the area, of a triangle, when A, = 4 cm, b = 8 cm, and gamma, is equal to 20 degrees_(0030).

 

Area, = 1 over 2, times 4, times 8, times, sine 20 degrees, = 5.47 cm, squared(0031).

Pythagoras Identity(0032).

sine, squared theta, plus cos squared, theta =1(0033).

 

Can be used, to get all the trig terms, to the same type(0034).

 

b0010

 

Proof(0035).

 

We use, the pythagorean theory,

A, squared, plus b squared, = c squared(0036).

 

Now we divide by, c squared,

A, squared, over c squared, plus b squared, over c squared, = c squared, over c squared(0037).

 

b0011

 

We get_,

A, squared, over c squared, plus b squared, over c squared, = 1(0038).

 

From the ratio of , the sides of a triangle, we can write this as,

sine, squared theta, plus cos squared, theta =1(0039).

 

A good way to think of this is,

opposite squared , plus adjacent squared , = hypotenuse squared, = opposite squared, over hypotenuse squared, plus adjacent squared, over hypotenuse squared, =1(0040).

 

 

b0012

A Practical Example(0041).

 

Find all the values of x, between 0 radians and 2 pie, radians, that satisfy the relationship,

15 sine, squared x, = cos x, plus 13(0042).

 

Using the Pythagoras Identity, we get,

15, 1 minus cos squared, x = cos x, plus 13(0043).

 

Now we can simplify,

15, cos squared, x minus cos x, minus 2, = 0(0044).

 

b0013

 

It is more convenient, to replace cos x, with u,

15, u squared, minus u, minus 2, = 0(0045).

 

Then we factor, the expression,

5u, plus 2, 3 u, minus 1, = 0(0046).

 

 

cos x, = minus 2, over 5, or 1 over 3(0047).

 

 

b0014

In order to determine what x is, we need to use,  cos, minus 1 x, on our calculators(0048).

 

cos, minus 1, minus 2, over 5, = 1.9823, radians(0049).

 

cos, minus 1, 1, over 3, = 1.2310, radians(0050).

 

b0015

 

But we need to remember that, in the interval, 2 pie, the cosine function, will be the same at, 2 pie, minus x(0051).

 

2 pie radians, minus 1.2310 radians, = 5.0222 radians(0052).

 

2 pie radians, minus 1.9823 radians, = 4.3009 radians(0053).

 

So, the complete answer is, 1.2310 radians, 1.9823 radians, 4.3009 radians, and 5.0222 radians(0054).

Tangent Identity(0055).

 

Can be used, to get all the trig terms, to the same type(0056).

 

tan theta, = sin theta, over cos theta_(0057).

 

b0017

 

Proof,

tan theta, = A, over b(0058).

 

Then we can divide, both the numerator, and the denominator, by c,

tan theta, = A, over c, over b, over c(0059).

 

We can write this as,

tan theta, = sin theta, over cos theta(0060).

 

b0018

 

Example(0061).

 

sin x, = 4 cos x, solve for sin x, all units, are in radians(0062).

 

We divide both sides, by cos x, and we get the identity,

tan x, = 4(0063).

 

We use, tan minus 1, x, to get that,  x = 1.3258 radians(0064).

 

Now we can solve for sin x,

sin x, = 4 cos, 1.3258 radians = 4 times 0.2425 radians, = 0.9701 radians(0065).
