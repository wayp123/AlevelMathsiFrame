Multiplication(0001).

 

To multiply polynomials, you simply multiply all the terms, in one, by all the terms, in the other, and then sum the results(0002). This method is known as the FOIL Method(0003). It stands for First Outer, then Inner Last(0004).

 

b0002

 

For example:,

3 x 2 plus 2x times 2 x 2 minus 5, can be broken down into(0005).

 

Multiply the First terms together,

3 x 2 times 2 x 2=6 x 4(0006).

 

Then multiply the Outer terms together,

3 x 2 time neg 5 = neg 15 x 2(0007).

 

Then multiply the Inner terms together,

2x times 2 x 2=4 x 3(0008).

 

Then multiply the Last terms together,

2x times neg 5 = neg 10x(0009).

 

b0003

 

Then we add the results together to give us,

6x4 plus 4x3 minus 15x2 minus 10x, written in descending powers(0010). As you become confident with the process, youll be able to do the entire multiplication in one go, without splitting it up(0011).

 

like this:,

3 x 2 plus 2x times 2 x 2 minus 5 = 6 x 4 plus 4 x 3 minus 15 x 2 minus 10x(0012).
