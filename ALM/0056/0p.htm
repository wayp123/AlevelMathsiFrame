Definite Integrals_(0001).

The definite integral, is used to find the area, underneath a curve, between two points(0002). The second part, of the fundamental theorem of calculus, allows us to evaluate these integrals, the resultant will be a number(0003).

 

The Definite Integral, is denoted as,

 

A, to b, f x, d x_(0004).

 

In the definite integral, A, is the lower limit, and b, is the upper limit, together, they are known as, the limits of integration(0005). The limits of integration, are the interval, over which, to find the area, else the area is infinite, under the infinite length, of a curve(0006). When we evaluate a definite integral, we dont write the constant c, because they will always cancel out, due to the subtraction operation, and are also liable to cause confusion(0007).

When we write,

 

f x, A, to b, this means that, we have found the indefinite integral, and are going to find the definite integral, from A, to b(0008).

A, to b, f x, d x, = F b, minus f A, where

 A, to b, f x, d x, = minus b, to A, f x, d x(0011).

5, The area between a curve, and the y-axis is,

 

A, to b, x, d y, for x, greater than 0(0014).
