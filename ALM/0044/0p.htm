Remainder Theorem_(0002).

 

The remainder theorem states that: If you have a polynomial, fx divided by x plus c, the remainder is equal to,  f, negative c(0003).

 

Here is an example, What, will the remainder be, if

x cubed, plus 8x squared, minus 4x squared, plus 17x, minus 40, is divided by, x minus 3?(0004).

 

fx becomes,  f, negative c_(0005). We substitute, x, with negative c, = Positive 3, for this example(0006).

 

f 3, = 3 cubed, plus 8, 3 squared, minus 4, 3 squared, plus 17, 3, minus 40, = 74(0007).

 

The remainder is 74(0008).

Factorising(0009).

 

When you factor an equation, you try to unmultiply the equation(0010). The N Roots Theorem, states that, if fx is a polynomial, of degree greater than, or equal to 1, then fx has exactly n, roots, providing that a root of, multiplcity k, is counted k times(0011). The last part means that, if an equation has 2 roots, that are both 6, then we count 6, as 2 roots(0012).
