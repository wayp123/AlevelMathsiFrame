Completing the square_(0001). (9980).

 

Changing a quadratic, to the completed square form, makes it easy to find several things, such as the roots of the quadratic, and the vertex of the quadratic, without even requiring a sketch(0007).

 

Completing the square is the process of changing, or rearranging a quadratic from the form of(0002).

 

A, x squared plus bx plus c, to the, equivalent form of(0003).

 

A, x plus d, squared plus e(0004).

 

For example, the quadratic,

2, x squared plus 8x plus 2 would become(0005).

 

2,x plus 2, squared minus 6(0006).

 

b0002

 

Here are the steps for completing the square(0008). Dont worry, its easier than it looks(0009).

 

b0003

 

Step 1,

Ensure the quadratic is in the conventional form of, A, x squared plus bx plus c(0010).

 

Example,

2 x squared plus 8x plus 2(0011).

 

 

General case,

A, x squared plus bx plus c_(0012).

 

b0004

 

Step 2,

Unless A, = 1, pull, or factor out A, that is, divide the entire quadratic by A, and put it outside a bracket, Note: If the quadratic is part of an equation you can divide each side by A, for example 2x2 plus 8x plus 2 = 0, simply becomes x2 plus 4x plus 1 = 0(0013).

 

b0005 

 

Example,

2x squared plus 4x plus 1(0014).

 

General case,

A, x squared plus b over A, x plus c over A(0015).

 

b0006

Step 3,

Replace, the x squared, plus kx part, with x plus k, over 2, squared(0016). Note, k now also = b, over A, due to the previous step(0017). It is important to realise that x plus k over 2 squared, is not equal to, but close to, the original expression(0018). This will be corrected in the next step(0019). To prevent writing something that isnt actually equal, it is a good idea to do both these steps at once, in your working, once you have got used to the method(0020). It is important to realise that after expanding x plus k over 2, squared, we get the original term plus an error of, k squared over 4(0021).

 

b0007

 

Example,

2x squared plus 4x plus 1 becomes 2, x plus 2 squared plus 1(0022).

 

General case,

A, x squared, plus b over A, x, plus c over A, becomes A, x plus, b over 2A, squared plus c over A(0023).

 

b0008

Step 4,

Correct the error introduced in the previous step by inserting, the subtraction, of a suitable number(0024). This suitable number can be found in two ways(0025). 1 By expanding the term inserted in the previous step, and comparing its magnitude to the original;, or 2, remembering that the error is always k squared over 4(0026). This step is known as completing the square, and gives the method its name(0027).

 

b0009

 

Example 1(0028).

x plus 2 squared, = x2 plus 4x plus 4, which is 3, greater than the original, x2 plus 4x plus 1, so neg 3 is inserted, to keep the value the same, as the original(0040). 2, the error is, k squared over 4, = 4 squared over 4, = 4, Subtract, or Change the sign of the error, and add, to the last term, to compensate, so 1 minus 4 = neg 3(0029).

 

b0010

Note,

 k = b over A, and the correction factor is,  minus k, squared over 4, or minus b squared over, 4A squared(0030).

 

General case,

A, x plus b over, 2A, squared minus b squared over, 4 A, squared plus c over, A(0031).     

 

b0011

 

Step 5,

If step 2 was necessary, then simplify the result, by expanding, or removing the outer bracket(0032). Then you are finished(0033).

 

b0012

 

Example of the result,

2x plus 2 squared minus 6(0034).

 

General case,

A, x plus b over 2A, squared minus b squared over 4A, plus c(0035). This is the general formula for completing the square(0036). This gives us the resultant form of

A, x plus d, squared, plus e(0041).

 

 

b0013 

 

Step 6_,

Check that what you have obtained expands back to what you started with, Note, you may feel confident to skip step 6(0037).
