The quadratic formula_(0001). (9979).

 

The quadratic formula is simply derived from the completing of the square formula, and rearranging it, to find x(0002).

 

The quadratic formula is_,

x= minus b plus minus the square root of b squared minus, 4 A, c over, 2A(0003).

 

It can be used to find the roots of a quadratic by putting numbers directly into it(0004).

 

b0002

For example,

 

y = 2x squared plus 8x plus 2(0005).

 

x= minus 8 plus minus the square root of 64 minus 16, over 4, = minus 2, plus, minus the square root of 3(0006).

 

so, 

x= minus 2 plus the square root of 3, and x= minus 2, minus the square root of 3(0007).

 

The factors of the equation are,

x plus 2, minus the square root of 3, and x plus 2, plus the square root of 3(0008).

 

Here is the way that the quadratic formula is derived from the completing the square formula(0009).

 

 

b0003

 

Step 1,

To solve an equation in the form of A x squared plus bx plus c = 0, first complete the square using the method above(0010). We could also use the quadratic formula, which is easier to use, but we must 1st derive it here(0011).

     

b0004

Example,

2x plus 2 squared minus 6=0 is a quadratic equation in completed square form(0012).

 

The General Case of the completing the square equation is,

A, x plus b over, 2 A, squared minus b squared over, 4 A, plus c=0(0013). Note that the minus b squared over, 4 A, plus c, term can be simplified to a constant term(0014).

 

 

b0005 

 

Step 2,

Start to rearrange by Isolating the x plus k squared term(0015).

 

 

b0006

Example,

2x plus 2 squared minus 6=0 becomes, 2x plus 2 squared =6 becomes, x plus 2 squared =3 (0016).

 

General Case,

The complete the square formula becomes, A, x plus b over, 2 A, squared, = b squared over 4 A, minus c, becomes, x plus b over 2 A, squared, = b squared over 4 A, squared, minus c over A(0017).

 

 

 

b0007

 

Step 3,

Square root each side, including a plus, and minus sign, as the part inside the bracket might be negative, or positive(0018).

 

b0008 

Example,

x plus 2= plus minus the square root of 3(0019).

 

General Case,

x plus b over 2 A, = plus minus the square root of b squared over 4 A, squared, minus c over A(0020).

 

 

b0009

 

Then some simplification(0021).

 

x plus b over 2 A, = plus minus the square root of b squared over 4 A, squared, minus 4 A c over 4 A, squared(0022).

 

x plus b over 2 A, = plus minus the square root of b squared, minus 4 A c over 4 A, squared(0023).

 

x plus b over 2 A, = plus minus the square root of b squared, minus 4 A c over, the square root of 4 A, squared(0024).

 

 

b0010

 

x plus b over 2 A, = plus minus the square root of b squared, minus 4 A c over 2A(0025).

 

b0011

 

Step 4,

Isolate x(0026).

 

 

b0012

Example,

x= plus minus the square root of 3 minus 2, or x= plus square root 3 minus 2, or minus square root 3 minus 2, x is equivalent to minus 0.268, or minus 3.73(0027).

 

General Case,

x= plus minus the square root of b squared minus 4 A, c over 2 A, minus b over, 2 A, Therefore the quadratic equation is x= minus b plus minus the square root of b squared minus 4 A c over, 2A(0028).
