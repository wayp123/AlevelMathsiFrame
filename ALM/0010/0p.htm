Polynomials_(0001). (9979).

 

You are already familiar with expressions such as,

3x plus 5, or 2x, 2 plus 8x plus 2(0002).

 

and perhaps,

x3 plus 2x minus 2(0003).

 

The general word for an expression that can be written in this form is a polynomial(0004). Excluding the constant term, every power of x, in a polynomial, is a positive integer(0005). Expressions that have x, to a non-integer, or negative power, are not polynomials(0006).

b0003 

 

Basics of polynomials(0027).

 

All polynomials can be written in the form:,

A0 plus A1,x, plus A2,x2, plus A3,x3, plus A4,x4, plus A n, x-N, and so on(0028).

 

The numbers,

A0,A1,A2,A3,A4, and so on, are known as the co-efficients, and will often already be known(0029).

 

For example in,

4x,2 plus 3x plus 9, the coefficient of x2, is 4(0030). The co-efficients may be 1, or even 0(0031).

 

for example_,

x3 plus x, is still a polynomial(0032).

 

b0004

 

Degrees of polynomials(0033).

 

The number, n, that is the highest power in the polynomial, is known as the degree, or sometimes the order, of the polynomial(0034).

 

b0005

 

For example__,

x4 plus 4x,2 plus 9, is a degree 4 polynomial(0035). Certain degrees have names, and are often written using certain letters for the coefficients when they are unknown:(0036).

 

Degree 0 or Constant, c, or k(0037).

Degree 1 or Linear,A x plus b, or mx plus c(0038).

Degree 2 or Quadratic, A, x squared plus bx plus c(0039).

Degree 3 or Cubic,A x cubed plus bx squared plus cx plus d(0040).

Degree 4 or Quartic,A x4 plus bx cubed plus cx squared plus dx plus e(0041).

 

b0006

 

Notation(0042).

 

Most of the polynomials on this page are written in terms of x, for example,

4x,2 plus 2x minus 9(0043).

 

Polynomials can be written with other letters,

3z2, plus z, minus 2, for example, is called a polynomial in z(0044).

 

b0007

 

It is conventional to write polynomials in descending powers for clarity, although a polynomial may have the powers in any order(0045). By writing polynomials in descending powers it is possible to see what the degree of the polynomial is, simply by looking at the first term(0046). Performing operations with two, or more polynomials, are also much simpler when the powers are in order, since like powers are usually grouped during calculations(0047). When working with polynomials, write in standard form, with missing terms, as 0 coefficients (0062).

 

Indeed, you may lose marks in your exam if you dont simplify things like,

x2 plus 5x minus 3x, to, x2 plus 2x(0048).

 

 

b0008

 

Operations with polynomials(0049).

 

Addition_(0050).

 

To add polynomials you simply add the co-efficients of each term(0051). If that sounds confusing dont worry as you will probably already know how to do this, it is basically the same as collecting like terms(0052). You add the co-efficients of x together, the co-efficients of x2 together, and so on(0053).

 

b0009

 

For example:,

4x,2 plus 2x plus 9 plus 6x,2 minus 2 = 10,x2 plus 2x plus 7(0054).

 

Notice that 4 plus 6 = 10, and 9 minus 2 = 7(0055).

When using this method it is essential that terms with x, to the same power, are lined up, leaving spaces if necessary as in the example above(0056).

 

b0010 

 

Subtraction_(0057).

 

For subtraction of polynomials, it is possible to use the same method as above, except subtracting instead of adding(0058). It may get confusing when there are negative terms involved, so it is preferred to reverse the signs in the second row, and then add the two polynomials together(0059).
