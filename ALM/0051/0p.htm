Angular Measurement, and Circular Sectors(0002).

 

A section, of a circle, is known as a sector, it has 3 sides, like a triangle(0003). Two sides, of the sector, is the radius, the other portion, that includes, part of the cirumference, is known as an arc(0004).

A circle has 360 degrees(0006). Each degree, can be divided, into one type of fraction, of 60 minutes, designated as,   and each minute, can have 60 seconds, designated as (0007). Angular Radian(0014).

 

One radian, is the angle, subtended, at the centre of a circle, by an arc segment, with its circumference, set equal in length, to the radius of the circle(0015). Since, we know that, the formula, for the total circumference, of the circle is C, = 2 pie r, we can say that there are  2 pie radians, in the circle(0016). Note, the r, for radius, cancels out, because we divide the circumference, with r, to get the number of radians, in a circle(0017). We abbreviate radians as rad(0018).

Conversion Between Degrees and Radians(0019).

 

Mathematics, requires us, to use radians, for most angular measurements, because the angle, is expressed as, _or simplified_ as, an arc length(0020). Therefore, we need to know, how to convert, from degrees, to radians(0021).

Since, we know, that there are, 360 degrees, or  2 pie, radians in a circle(0022). We can determine these equations(0023).

 

1 degree, = pie, over 180, radians(0024).

 

1 radian, = 180 over pie, degrees(0025).

So we can write these general formulae(0026).

 

x degrees, = x, pie, over 180, radians(0027).

 

x radians, = x, 180, over pie, degrees(0028).

 

Here is an example, to convert  20 degrees, into radians(0029).

20 degrees, = 20, pie over 180, = 1 over 9, pie, radians(0030).

Convert, , 1 over 9, pie, radians, into degrees(0031).

1 over pie, radians, = 1 over 9, pie, 180 over pie, = 20 degrees(0032).

Arc Length_(0033).

 

In most cases, it is very difficult, to measure the length of an arc, with a ruler(0034). Therefore we need to use a formula, in order to determine, the length of the arc(0035). The formula that we use is(0036).

 

arc length, = theta, in radians, times the radius(0037).

 

in symbols this is,

s= theta, r(0038).

 

So for 2 pie, radians, the arc length, is the circumferance, c, = 2, pie, r(0039).

Here is an example, determine the length, of the arc, created by a sector, with a 6cm radius, and an angle of, 53 degrees_(0040).

 

The first thing we need to do is convert, from degrees, to radians(0041).

53 degrees, = 53 pie, over 180, radians(0042).

 

 

Now we can calcuate the length of the arc,

s, = 53 pie, over 180, times 6cm, = 5.55cm(0043).

Area of a Sector_(0044).

 

The area of a sector, can be found using this formula(0045).

 

Area, = 1 over 2, r squared, theta(0046).

 

Note: theta needs to be in radians, and the area of the whole circle, with sector length, of 2 pie, is pie, r, squared(0047). 

 

Calculate, the area of a sector, with a 3cm radius, and an angle of 2 pie_(0048).

Area, = 1 over 2, 3 2, times 2 pie, = 28.3cm(0049).
