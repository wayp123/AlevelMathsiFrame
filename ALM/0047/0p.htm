Binomial expressions_(0001).

A binomial, is a polynomial, with two parts, in the form, A, plus b, such as,x plus 1_(0002). When a binomial, is raised to a power, you could simplify it, by multiplying out the brackets, several times(0003). The expanded polynomial, is called a binomial expansion, and all binomial expansions, follow a pattern, that can be used, to expand binomials, quicker than multiplying out several brackets(0004). For now, we will only look at binomial expressions, which are raised to, positive integers(0005).

Binomial coefficient formula_(0014).

 

When a binomial is raised to a large power, it may be too time consuming, to find the binomial coefficients, by writing out, Pascals triangle(0015). Fortunately, there is a formula, that can find any line, of Pascals triangle(0016).

 

If n, is the power of the expansion, and r, is the number of the term, in a single row, the binomial coefficient formula is(0017).

 

n, r, = n factorial, over r factorial, n minus r, factorial(0018).

 

The_  exclamation, means factorial_, and multiplies n, by every integer, less than itself, down to 1(0019).

 

b0005

 

So_,

3 factorial, = 3 times 2 times 1, = 6(0020).

 

 

To find the binomial coefficients, you use the formula, with the required value of n, and r = 0, r = 1, r = 2, and so on, until r = n(0021).

 

Most scientific calculators, will have two buttons, that will be useful in this process, one is the factorial button, usually labelled n!, and the other, will actually find, n r, and is often labelled  cnr, or cnr(0022).  The C, stands for choose, or combination, which is based, on the formulas use, in probability(0023).
