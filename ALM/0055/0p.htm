Integration_(0001).

Integration, is the process of anti-differentiation(0002). In the last segment, we saw that, differentiating a function, gives the gradient _or rate of change_ of that function(0003). Integrating a function, on the other hand, gives the area, underneath the curve of that function(0004).

Basic integration(0005).

Integration, is the reverse, of differentiation(0006).

 

Notation(0007).

The integral sign is,

 f x, d x(0008).

 

This symbol was chosen by Leibniz, because it looks like an elongated S, and an integral, is a limit, of sums(0009). The_  f x, is known as the integrand(0010). _The_ d x, means that we integrate, with respect to x(0011).

The Fundamental Theorem, of Calculus(0025).

This is the most important rule, in calculus, it establishes that, differentiation, and integration, are inverse processes(0026). Here is the theorem, Let f, be continuous, on the inclusive interval [a, b](0027).

 

 1, if g x, = A, to x, f t, d t, then, g dash x, = f x(0028).

 

2, A, to b, f x, d x, = f b, minus f A(0029).

The first rule states that, if we integrate a function, and then we differentiate the resultant, then we will arrive at, the same function(0031). The second part says, that we can find a definite integral, by subtracting the value, of f, at the endpoints(0032).

Indefinite Integrals(0034).

 

The first part, of the Fundamental theorem of calculus, establishes that, if we differentiate a function, and then integrate a function, we get a indefinite integral(0035). When we evaluate an indefinite integral, the resultant will be a function(0036). For an indefinite integral, we use the appropriate rule, to get the general antiderivative(0037). If a point on the graph is given, or we can choose a point, say at x=0, we can solve for C, to get the complete antiderivative(0038).
